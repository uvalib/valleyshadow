Records Related to Augusta County Regiments


From: GEO. B. McCLELLAN, Maj.-Gen., Commanding.
July 17 1861.

Summary:
Union General George B. McClellan, commanding forces in West Virginia during July, 1861, writes General in Chief Winfield Scott. McClellan asks Scott if he should advance on Staunton.

Lieut. Gen. WINFIELD SCOTT:

BEVERLY,

July 17 1861.

Will a movement of mine on Staunton facilitate your plans? If so, I can probably take that position. I do not know your plans of operation, but can move on Staunton if you desire.

Please reply at once.

GEO. B. McCLELLAN,
Maj.-Gen., Commanding.

Bibliographic Information : Letter Reproduced from The War of The Rebellion: Official Records of the Union and Confederate Armies, Series 1, Volume 2, Serial No. 2, Pages 743, Broadfoot Publishing Company, Wilmington, NC, 1997.
