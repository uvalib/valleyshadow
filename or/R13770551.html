Records Related to Augusta County Regiments


From: T. M. HARRIS, Col., Cmdg.
May 28, 1864.

Summary:
Union Colonel T. M. Harris reports to General Benjamin Kelley that Confederate troops passed through Staunton on their way to join Lee in May, 1864.

Brig.-Gen. KELLEY:

BEVERLY,

May 28, 1864.

My own scouts, who have been beyond Franklin, and Gen. Hunter's scouts, who came through from Cedar Creek and arrived last night, concur in this statement. The rebel forces passed through Staunton on 21st, going to join Lee. Have written you the particulars. There is no doubt of its correctness.

T. M. HARRIS,
Col., Cmdg.

Bibliographic Information : Letter Reproduced from The War of The Rebellion: Official Records of the Union and Confederate Armies, Series 1, Volume 37, Serial No. 70, Pages 551, Broadfoot Publishing Company, Wilmington, NC, 1997.
