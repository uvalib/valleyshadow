HIUS 403: Digital History and the American Civil War
 
 
Franklin Co. Home Front: The Burning of Chambersburg
 
 
TEAM MEMBERS:
Elizabeth Crocker
Raj Doshi
Kevin McMurphy
Henry Tran
 
Starter Index

This team will build a web site on the Franklin Co. Home Front, concentrating on the summer of 1864 and the burning of Chambersburg. The burning of the city left a remarkable record of Chambersburg life in the form of claims filed with the state of Pennsylvania. These claims will comprise one of the main sources for this team. There are just over 400 claims on microfilm and they should be put into a simple database format for searching and indexing. In addition, this team will consider questions of community and war, Southern motivation for the burning, and local reaction. The literature of the Civil War includes a great deal of writing on the idea and practice of total war. The war-making of Sherman, Jackson, Grant, and others have been examined as the roots of 20th century total war. This team will have the opportunity to view this kind of war-making at the local level and judge its character, objectives, devastation, and implications.
