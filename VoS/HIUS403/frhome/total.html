Sherman and Hunter, in their raids of the South, brought the battlefront into the homes of Southern civilians. McCausland began a new phase in the war against the North when he burned Chambersburg to the ground. By 1864, generals on both sides of the Mason Dixon Line were more willing to disregard the "rules of war" than they had been before. Total war, which merged homefront and battlefront, became more of the order of the day.
