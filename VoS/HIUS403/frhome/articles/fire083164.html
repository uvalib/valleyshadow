The Valley Spirit, August 31, 1864
BURNING OF CHAMBERSBURG
Firing the Town

They appeared to have adopted a systematcally arranged plan for burning the town by firing in regular otder from the center to the suburbs. The first house fired was alarge unfinished building, belongin to Mr. Peter Brough, situated a few doors fown from the Diamond. It was full of combustible material, over which they poured coal-on and applied the torch. It soon had the surrounding houses and the Arcade Buildings opposite in flames. The next place fired was the Grocery Store of Mr. Wm. Gelwicks on the Diamond and about the same time the Court House, and in ten minutes after after flames were seen to insue from almost everu house along Main and Market streets. No person can describe the scene of terror and confuscion that ensue among the inhabitants.
