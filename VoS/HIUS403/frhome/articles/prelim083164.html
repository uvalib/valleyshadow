The Burning of Chambersburg
Valley Spirit, August 31, 1864
THE BURNING OF CHAMBERSBURG
Preliminary Remarks

It is not our purpose to go into any minute details of the circumstances of our once beautiful and happy town. So thick and fast do extraordinary events transpire that the Burning of Chambersburg is already an old story-save to those who passed through the ordeal of that dreadful day. From their minds its horrors can never be effaced.-There are, however, some circumstances connected with this unparalled act of vandalism that we deem most important to notice in order to correct injurious impressions abroad in regard to the position of our citizens in the affair; and also to show up the military "situation" at the Headquarters of the Department of the Susquehanna at the date of occurrence.
