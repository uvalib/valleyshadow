Chambersburg society changed significantly during the years of the Civil War. Women faced a very different lifestyle than they ever had. The church had to adjust to deal with the realities of war that confronted Chambersburg citizens. The wartime economy boomed as Chambersburg became a tranportaion hub for military operations, but the war also made some people unable to pay their debts, especially after raids by Stuart and Lee. These changes, and the parts of life that remained the same, illustrate the picture of a home front society.
