Letter

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen, and Abandoned Lands
Office 4th Division, 9th Sub-District, Virginia
Staunton, Va., Sept. 30, 1867

Capt. John A. McDonnell
Sub. Asst. Comm.
Winchester

Captain

I have the honor to enclose a claim of Joshua Harris (col'd) against Mr. Jaccheri of Charlottesville (who kept a restaurant at White Sulphur Springs) amount $18.75

Jaccheri has paid Harris nothing and makes as I understand no excuse except that six (6) Knives & forks were lost.

Harris claims that even if the knives were lost the room was open day & night by order of Mr. Jaccheri and their value is very far below $18 75/100. Harris is a faithful, respectable man and bears a first class character for honesty.

Yr Obt Srvt
Thos. P. Jackson
Agent

One Enclosure

Attachments

Joshua Harris was also involved in a wage dispute with Annie E. Branham of Staunton.
