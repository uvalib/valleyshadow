Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Office 4th Division, 9th Sub-District, Virginia.
Staunton, Va., March 6 1868

Capt. John A. McDonnell
Sub Asst Comm
Winchester

Captain
I have the honor to transmit herewith enclosed paper in reference to claim of Henry Alexander's heirs (col'd) against estate of J. McPhetus McCutchen, deceased, asking the matter be referred to Military Com 14th Divn. for investigation. Benj. Moten came here from Fredericksburg but found legal expences [sic] too much for him to prosecute the claim and further he could not absent himself from his business in Fredericksburg. The other claimants who live here are too poor to sue in court. The estate is solvent, and the adminstrators a loyal honorable man who is will admit the claim if proper proof is furnished, and a recipt given which will be admitted as a valid voucher in his settlement of accounts with the estate.

Very Respectfully
Yr. Obt Servt
Thos. P. Jackson
Asst. Sub Asst Comm
