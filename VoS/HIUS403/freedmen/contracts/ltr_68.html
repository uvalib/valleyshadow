Letter

Waynesboro Feb'y 20th 1868
Mr. Th. P. Jackson

Dear Sir You did not come on Tuesday as you promised me. Those negroes are still in their [sic] and want possession of the house. As I told I gave them 3 weeis notice one I hired only by the week and settle up with her the other one has been staying their she has good opportunities to hire for more than I gave her but she thinks I think that she will draw a support from the government but she is able support herself [sic] You will accomdate [sic] me by removing them immediately and you oblige yours &c.

John K. Keizh

[Attachment indicates that the black woman who will not vacate the premises is named Louisa Washington]
