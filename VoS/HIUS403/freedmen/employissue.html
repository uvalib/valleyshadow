Employment Issues

The Freedmen's Bureau in Staunton was involved both in the placement of laborers and in the termination of labor relationships. White employers wrote to the Bureau seeking farmhands, cooks, nannies, and other laborers. They also wrote to the Bureau seeking advice in dismissing laborers, and displayed some anxiety as to handle the termination of the freedmen.

White employers also wrote to the Bureau to dispute the claims of the freedmen they had employed, demonstrating the difficulty the Bureau had in ensuring that freedmen were entering into clearly contracted labor situations. The arguments that arise in the letters sent to the Bureau indicate that too frequently, the Bureau only became involved in employment disputes after a contract had been negotiated and its terms came into contention.

Another issue that seems to have occupied the Bureau's attention is pressure placed upon the freedmen by their employers to vote for candidates approved by the white population, demonstrating another pressure placed upon the freedmen as they struggled to assert themselves as free citizens without dangerously alienating the white population that still controlled so much of their lives.
