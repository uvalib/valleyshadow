Valley Virginian


"UNIVERSAL AMNESTY"
vol. 1, no. 17, March 28, 1866, pg. 3, col. 3
The Charlottesville Chronicle says: "The strict impertinent thing of the session is the late resolution of the Conservative Senator Steward, of Nevada, for universal suffrage and universal amnesty as a compromise.-- We may save our necks and get into Congress by universal negro suffrage; this from a Conservative Republican. We suppose the Radical ground is, suffrage to the negroes in the South, and to nobody else. -- Senator Steward offers this as an olive- branch to the South, for the consideration of each State.

This reminds us of two negroes who got into a fight, and the big one beat the little one almost to death, who hallooed "nuff." They got upon their feet, when Ceasar seuzed Pompey (the little nigger) firmly by the collar, and addressed him thus: "Now, Pomp, I don't want any bad feelings atixt us, and I pose to comp'mise --just take orf your shirt, old fellar, and I'll hit you, Pomp, 150 lashes--and then you can put on your shsirt again and go about your business--and no questions axed --I offer you, Pomp, a universle amnesty."
