Captain

I respectfully acknowledge receipt of your communication (personal) under date Ap 22/67 by requesting me to "report the time from which his (Mr Tukey's) sevices commenced immediately" and "if a continuances of his service are necessary (that I) you will make application to this (your) office to" Mr. Tukey has performed no services in connecton with the Bureau business of this office since my arrival here and is now absent from Staunton, as several times during this month, on private buisines. If the permission to employ extends to "one month only I see an objection reporting him as clerk although the business of this office is much in arrear as regards the claim of Freedmen and with the additional duites of Registrar accepted by me, I shall hardly be able alone to satisfy myself. So far I cannot find in either Magisteral Dist, if Stuanton a qualified for Registring officer who can talk the present adn my present intention is to recommend Mr Tukey as a proper person for the position.

Your Obdt Svt
Tho P Jackson
Agent
