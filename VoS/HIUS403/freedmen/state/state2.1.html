Staunton Spectator


This piece was excerpted from the Page Valley Courier and printed in the Staunton Spectator on January 1, 1868
An Industrious Negro--Timothy Dixon, colored man, living in this county, informs us that he has put out during the past season one hundred acres of wheat and seven acres of rye, assisted by only one hane. He has a prospect for a fine crop nxt summer. It would be much better if all the colored people would follow his example, than lying around the towns and cities of the state.
