Claims Against the Government

Claims against the government included personal property, crops, livestock, and just about anything else that the U.S. army could use while camped in Staunton. From the dates of the claims submitted to the Bureau, it is evident that claimants could wait many years to be reimbursed. Many claims were sent to the Bureau, apparently because it was a local arm of the federal government that seemed more accessible than sending a claim to a faceless official in Washington. Despite its proximity, claimants would discover, the Bureau had no power to aid local citizens in recouping their losses.

  • Alexander Anderson filed his complaint with the Bureau in 1867 for losses sustained in 1864.
