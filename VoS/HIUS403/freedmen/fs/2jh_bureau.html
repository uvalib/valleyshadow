Bureau Correspondances
  • November 29, 1867: Agent Thomas P. Jackson introduces the case to Assisstant Commissioner John A. McDonnell and requests that inquiry be made to find James Harris.
  • December 2, 1867: John A. McDonnell refers the case to Assistant Commissioner of the Virginia state Bureau.
  • December 4, 1867: O Brown refers the case to Agent R.S. Lacey for investigation.
  • December 6, 1867: R.S. Lacey forward the case to Agent Andrew Mahony for investigation.
  • December 28, 1867: Thomas P Jackson requests information about actions taken on the case and forwards the only letter recieved by James Harris' wife, Elizabeth, since his departure.
  • January 6, 1868: John A McDonnell requests information about the state of the case and forward the letter from James Harris which was addressed to his wife.
  • January 9, 1868: Andrew Mahony reports his findings to R.S. Lacy.
  • January 9, 1868: O Brown requests information from R.S. Lacey about stage of investigation on the case.
  • January 11, 1868: R.S. Lacy requests report from Andrew Mahony of the results of his investigation/
  • January 13, 1868: R.S. Lacy forwards the information from Andrew Mahony, dated January 9, 1868, to O Brown.
  • January 15, 1867: O Brown relays the information provided by Andrew Mahony on January 9, 1868 to John A. McDonnell
  • January 15, 1867: John A. McDonnell forwards the information from Andrew Mahony, dated January 9, 1868, to Thomas P. Jackson.
  • January 20, 1868: R.S. Lacey submits to O Brown the results of Andrew Mahoney's investigation.
  • January 23, 1868: O Brown forwards the results of the investigation to John A. McDonnell.
  • January 27, 1868: John A. McDonnell forwards Andrew Mahony's report to Thomas P. Jackson.
