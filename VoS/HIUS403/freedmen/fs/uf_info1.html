Attached Letter


Dec 5 1867
Capt John A McDonnell
Sub Asst Comm
Winchester

Captain
I respectfully ask enquiry be made at Richmond for a colored man known only here as "Uncle Fleming" said to live at "Lee's" jail, an old slave pen. If found, that he be asked to give the name and residence of the man named Quarles, who bought three of the children of Nancy Thornton, Reuben about 13 years old Amy 12 and ________ 11. Nacy is ery anxious to find her children and if Fleming can not be found at Lee's Jail I ask such enquiry be made for him as will deserve hime if in Richmond.

Very Respectfully
Yr. obt sevt
Tho P Jackson
Agent
