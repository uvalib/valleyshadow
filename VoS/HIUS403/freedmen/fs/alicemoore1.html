A 16 year old black girl by the name of Alice Moore requests the Bureau for help in finding her parents.

  • Bureau Record/Summary of case of Alice Moore
  • May 30, 1867: Thomas P. Jackson refers case to Capt. John A. McDonnell
  • Stuanton, Va
    May 30 1867

    Jackson Tho.P

    Writes on behalf of Alice Moore (Freed girl) asking that information be sought of her father Roderick Moore living with Wm Hayward Ham, 10 miles from Goldsboro N.C. and of her mother Sally Moore living with Nicholas Bess, 6 miles from Goldsboro. Alice was sent to Richmond and sold in 1864-5.

    (on official Stationary)

    May 30 1867

    Capt John A McDonnell
    Sub Asst Comm
    Winchester

    Captain

    Alice Moore aged 16 asks information of her parents Roderick Moore, who lived with Wm Hayward Ham 10 miles from Goldboro N.C. on Kinston raod, and Sally Moore who lived with Nicholas Bess 6 miles from Goldsboro. Ham sent the child to be sold in 1864c5 and she was bought by Wm McFall of Augsta County who lives with WM Shumate fo Stuanton. I respectfully ask enquiry be made through proper channel

    Yr. obt sevt
    Tho P Jackson
    Agent
