Valley Virginian


vol. 1, no. 15, March 14, 1866, pg. 1, col. 4
A negro sentenced to forty days confinement and labor with ball and chain, at Macon, was recently, allowed to ascent to the second story of the prison, from which he threw himself down. He struck on the top of his head, and was, of course, unhurt. In consideration of the feat he received a free pardon.
