Valley Virginian


vol. 2, no. 25, May 22, 1867, pg. 1, col. 7
The negroes who are employed in loading the Mississippi Transportation Company's barge, at Vioksburg, struck for higher wages a day or two ago. They were getting a dollar and a half a day, but demanded two dollars. The company, by construction of a plank road, managed to get drays up to the barge, and dispensed with the services of the negroes altogether, saving thereby at least $50 a day.
