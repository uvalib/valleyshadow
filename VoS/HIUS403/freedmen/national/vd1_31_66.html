AN AMENDMENT TO THE CONSTITUTION
vol. 1, no. 9, January 31, 1866, pg. 1, col. 7
The following amendment, to the Constitution of the U.S., has been introduced by Mr. Stevens, in the House, and by the joing Committee in the Senate. It met with some opposition but we suppose it will pass.

"Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of America, in Congress assembled, two-thirds of both houses concurring: That the following article be proporsed to the Legislatures of the several Staes a an amendment to the Constitution of the United States, which when adopted by three-fourths of the said Legislatures, shall be voted as a part of the Constitution:

"ARTICLE--Representative and direct taxes shall be apportioned among the several States which may be in the Union, according to their respective numbers, counting the whole number of persons in each State, exclusive Indians not taxed; provided, that whenever the elective franchise shall be denied or abridged in any State on account of race or color, all persons of such race or color shall be excluded from the basis of representation."
