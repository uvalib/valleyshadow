DeWitt Clinton Gallaher was born in Sheperdstown in Jefferson County, Virginia on August 2, 1845. As a youth he moved with his family to Waynesboro, Virginia. Gallaher was educated at Hampden-Sidney College, Washington College (now Washington and Lee University), and the University of Virginia.

He moved to Charleston, West Virginia following the war and began practicing law. On July 27, 1876 Gallaher married Florence Walton Miller. In 1919 Gallaher began compiling a booklet from the memo books and the scraps of paper that managed to survive the war. The experiences depicted in this diary reflect the period that Dewitt Clinton Gallaher served in the Confederate Army during the "War Between the States."

Gallaher served from the spring of 1863 until April 9, 1865. Originally, he was assigned as a captain and aide de camp to General Imboden's staff. Gallaher also served in Company E in the First Virginia Cavalry as a courier for General J.E.B. Stuart. He later served under General Fitzhugh Lee and General T.L. Rosser.

DeWitt Clinton Gallaher died in 1926.
