William Smith Hanger Baylor was born in Augusta County on April 7, 1831. He graduated from Washington College, and also studied at the University of Virginia. Baylor married Mary Hawes in 1859, and was the Commonwealth Attorney for Staunton. In 1858, residents of Staunton formed a local militia company, the West Augusta Guard, a division of the 160th Regiment Virginia Militia. Baylor was Captain of the Guard, which travelled to Alexandria and Charlestown in late 1859, in the wake of John Brown's raid. Baylor served in the 5th Virginia Infantry and in April of 1862 Baylor was made commander of the Stonewall Brigade. Baylor, mortally wounded at the Battle of Second Manassas, died on August 30, 1862. He was buried at Hebron Presbyterian Church Cemetery in Augusta County.

W.S.H. Baylor to his wife, April 22, 1862

Baylor describes being conferred the "Colonelcy" of the 5th Va., and his feelings about the duty that lies before him.

W.S.H. Baylor to his wife, August 18, 1862.

Baylor tells his wife about his struggles with military leadership, and his interactions with Generals Jackson and A.P. Hill.

Edward P. Walton to Mrs. Baylor, October 1, 1862.

Walton reports Colonel Baylor's death to Mrs. Baylor, and describes Baylor's religious conversion before he died.
