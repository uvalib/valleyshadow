Harvey Bear Diary

 

February 28, 1862

Went to church, this day was set apart by the President for humiliation and prayer, very windy and cold.

 

March 11, 1862

…notice came that the Militia must report themselves in Staunton on Friday next with two days rations to march immediately to Winchester.

 

March 14, 1862

The Militia ordered to report in Staunton, they returned in evening to report next morning, I attended the election all the time to day.

 

March 15, 1862

Went to Staunton for Fannie we returned in evening rained nearly all day, the militia ordered to report Monday, some men from our neighborhood disgraced themselves and families by running to the mountain, to avoid being drafted.

 

March 16, 1862

Sunday 16th went to church small congregation.

 

March 17, 1862

The 160 Regiment started for Strasburg where they have to report to Genl. Jackson. Mr. Dinkles little Maggie died poor child had to suffer immense sick some 20 days, she is to be buried to morrow 11 o’clock.

 

March 18, 1862

Helped dig grave.

 

March 25, 1862

…heard this evening Gen. Jackson had a battle five miles this side of Winchester on last Sunday heard he was repulsed with considerate losses, not known how many the Federals lost.

 

April 21, 1862

Rained all day and all night last night water high excitement not abated Genl. Johnson passed through Churchville today on his way to his army at West View took dinner with Mr. Lindsay and Rev. Mr. Emmerson at my home.

 

April 22, 1862

…Went part the way with Rebecca on her way to school …stopped at camp near West View found the soldiers in fine spirits.

 

April 23, 1862

…Great excitement concerning the movement of the enemy some of their scouts in Jennings Gap took Wm. Hite prisoner with two officers. Members of our cavalry in Churchville scouting the roads. We expect the enemy in here soon.

 

April 25, 1862

…Lent Capt. Hottle horse to ride to Mt. Solon. J.B. Sellars rode bay mare to Rockbridge to have her out of the way of the Federals, if they come in.

 

April 26, 1862

…Excitement still prevailing not knowing the excitement what the movement of our army will be, the evacuation of the Valley seems evident.

 

April 27, 1862

Went to church but few members, Bishop Glossbrenner preached at Churchville in evening large assembly. Sent 100 lbs Bacon to camp at Churchville. Clear weather.

 

April 28, 1862

Heard today the Federals leaving the Gaps. Gone across Shenandoah Mountain… Capt Hottle returned with horse.

 

May 5, 1862

Sold 71 lbs Bacon to Rockbridge Cavalry, 22 lbs to Charlotte Cavalry… Heard today Gen. Jackson bringing reinforcements to Staunton. Federals at McDowell, some this side of Shenandoah Mountain, expect an attack in a few days

 

May 7, 1862

… helped J.B. Sellars to plant corn. Col. Johnson passing with his army through the mountain.

 

May 8, 1862

Planting corn, very pretty weather, heard cannon from 12 to about 3 o’clock supposed to be the Federals on Bull Pasture Mountain.

 

May 16, 1862

Went to church very few persons there, piloted wagons across the river. In evening visited army in Jennings Gap, returned home at dark and found a lot of soldiers at my house for the night.

 

June 10, 1862

…Very little news today concerning the army. Genl Jackson repulsed the enemy on yesterday with considerable loss to enemy. Jackson now in Brown’s Gap awaiting reinforcements. Considerable of his men wounded.
