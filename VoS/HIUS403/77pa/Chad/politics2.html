The vast bulk of political articles ran in either paper from 1861-1865, however, were not so much an examination of the political soldier, but of the politics of the soldier. Posturing as the "true friend of the soldier" was frequently done by both parties, and usually found its ways into the print of the respective papers. After the 77th fought in McCook's Division at Shiloh, Gov. Curtin was lambasted in the Valley Spirit for not providing the soldiers with the proper acknowledgement and praise.
