July 29, 1863


The time is near at hand when the unconditional Union men of our county will meet in Convention to select a candidate to represent them in the State Legislature. In the present crisis of the country it is necessary that men of the highest mental and moral qualifications should be chosen. There are many citizens who believe that Col. F.S. Stumbaugh possesses in an eminent degree the character peculiarly requisite, in these times, in a legislator. The ability and honesty of the Colonel in civil life are well known throughout the county, and he has abundantly proved his devotion to his country in the present war. All know how he behaved and how nobly he sustained the honor of our State and county at Pittsburg Landing, and how ably he conducted the command of his brigade during a large portion of his term of service. It is well known, too, that should our enemies make an electioneering campaign necessary, the Colonel would be as brilliant a campaigner as he was on the field of death-dealing war.

While the war brought Stumbaugh success, it brought many politicians frustration and political grief.

The papers cried out for heroes like Stumbaugh to be duly recognized...
