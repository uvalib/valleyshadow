Andrew Brooks to Eleanor Stuart Brooks

Camp Winder
March 17th, 1863

Dear Ma,
Your letter by Jimmy Burns was received this evening. Charles' letter came yesterday, after looking for a letter for over a week. I had begun to think you had forgotten me, it was so long coming. He mentioned that Johnny had gone somewhere (but didn't say where) except that I would no doubt see him before I got his letter. He didn't tell me what he had gone for, or anything about it, nor did you except that he sent to Gen Lee a recommendation for my detail. What sort of a thing is it and who recommended me. I suppose he was in Richmond and took the contract to the war office. I haven't gotten the letter you say he wrote me and have never gotten but one letter from him since I left. I have been very anxious to hear from him and know what is doing at the furnace. It will now soon be time it was put in blast.
When I commenced writing I had just returned from preaching, which we have every night. Dr. Hoge and Mr. Lacy are gone. There is a chaplain with each regiment of the brigade, two Presbyterians and three Methodists. Lieut Culton has resigned-unable to stand infantry service. I don't know who will get his place, if his resignation is accepted.
We heard considerable firing across the river yesterday, but haven't heard the cause Wednesday morning. I will get the box today-am very much obliged to you all for it. I am not much afraid of it being much like what I get here, there is but one place I ever saw that I got such rations. I am sorry Miss Sue Harden is about to leave the neighborhood-young people are very scarce there. Emmett is on another trip to Uncle John's, wonder if he will get there this time. I heard Mary Susan was to atttend a small party at Dr. Dold's- a kind of farewell to him I suppose. I am very well. Love to all the friends.

As ever your son,

Andrew
