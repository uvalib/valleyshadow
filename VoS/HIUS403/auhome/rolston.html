The letters below have been excerpted from "Until Separated by Death," the correspondence of Jesse Rolston Jr. and Mary Catharine Cromer Rolston. The Rolstons and their five children lived in Mt. Solon, in the northern part of Augusta County. Jesse joined the Confederate Army in Staunton on July 16th, 1861, and did not return home permanently until June 3rd, 1865. Most of the letters in "Until Separated by Death" are from Jesse to Mary Catharine, but it also contains the letters of friends and family. Though few of Mary Catharine's letters to Jesse survive, the letters from Jesse and others give a unique perspective on her life at home, as well on on homefront life in general.

Ann Rolston to her sister July 28, 1861
Discusses the rumor that Jesse has volunteered

Jesse Rolston to Mary Catharine March 8, 1862
Sends money and mentions a man who would like to buy corn from Mary

Jesse Rolston to Mary Catharine April 3, 1863
Tells about camp life and advises which crops to plant

Jesse Rolston to Mary Catharine May 9, 1863
Sends messages to the children telling them to behave

Elizabeth Cromer to her sister May 26, 1864
Writes of family news and mentions wounded soldiers in Staunton

Jesse's sister to Mary Catharine February 21, 1864
Discusses miserable living conditions
