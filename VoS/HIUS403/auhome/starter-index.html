HIST 403: Digital History of the American Civil War

Augusta Home Units Team Starter Index

The Augusta Home Guard units were organized throughout the war to defend Staunton and the people of Augusta County. The main unit designations for these men were the 3rd Btn. Valley Reserves, Capt. Avis' Co. Provost Guard Staunton, and the Marquis' Boys Battery. These troops saw action at the Battle of Piedmont, where regular U.S. troops on June 5, 1864, routed the smaller, weaker Confederate forces. The victory allowed the U.S. forces to occupy Staunton for the first time in the war.

The Valley project has already researched and compiled their military dossier files. The team put the information concerning the 3rd Btn. into a searchable database. The team also has access to a searchable catalog of the two wartime Staunton newspapers, the Spectator and the Vindicator.

The men of the 3rd Btn. were of two types: the teenaged boy or the man in his late-40s. Most entered service for the first time on April 23, 1864--meaning the vast majority of the battalion were soldiers for no more than 44 days. If not soldiers, what were these men? Most were farmers in Augusta County, serving the war effort by trying to meet the great demands of the food supply for the CS Army. But more important and more widespread was the regional sense of impending danger, of the home front becoming the war front. And it did. We shall try to tell their story.
