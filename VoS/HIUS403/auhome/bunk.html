Camp at Bunkers Hill October the 5 1862

Deer Sisters
I receive your letter and was glad to hear from you all I was not in the hospitle the doctor said that I had a litle tuch of the fevor but I am nearly well now I did not get them eatings you said you had sent me I would like to got it for I have been nearly starved to deth John says when Baley Dunlap gets well that he ma[y] take too of an horses and bring a load down to us and him get the waggon and Wash and I can cum along with him and take the waggon back I was not in the fite in marland I was sick at the time I want you to ask Wash what he done with that letter and footry I wan[t] you to rite to me soon and tell me John Dull is liing by my side now with the fevor Wilson is going to try to send him home with Jim Calhoon in the waggon I want you to send me some peaches and pies and anney thing that is good to eat I have nothing more to rite to yo I recking you have got that horse Rit[e] to me and tel me al about that letter I have bee[n] one of the boys tha[t] you evry save I read the testament nearley evry day & nothing more at present but rite soon to your Brother Joseph Trimble

his Sister Jane Trimble
Sue Trimble
George Trimble
