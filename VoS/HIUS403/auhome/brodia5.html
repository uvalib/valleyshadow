Friday, March 28th:
Crowds of ladies and gentlemen daily assembled without the walls where a view could be had of the windows of the Jail and testified their sympathy and support by every demonstration in their power. Those known to sympathize with secessionists were prohibited admittance to the jail; but still they found many ways of ministering to our comfort and necessities, for which I (and I doubt not, all the prisoners) will ever feel most grateful. It was a cheering thought that there many thousands of our friends in the city, ready and anxious to assist with their means and cheer us with their sympathy in our lonely confinement. It was rumored that we would be transferred to Fort Delaware.
