Search the Valley of the Shadow's 1890 Franklin County Special Veterans Census

After the war, most men returned to their homes in Franklin County, where they resumed their lives as farmers and laborers. Although during the war they'd been forced to change their lives significantly, their post-war lives closely resemble their pre-war lives.

To find out more about these men's post-war lives, search the 1890 Franklin County Special Veterans Census. This census provides information about the men's military experience as well as general information about their lives in 1890. It is important to note that this census is not as reliable as the 1860 Franklin County Population Census. This census includes information about black and white soldiers from Franklin County, however this information was never established as fact. For example, in the 1890 Franklin County Special Veterans Census, Washington McAlver is listed as being in the 54th Massachusetts (Colored) Infantry. However, we found no compiled service record for McAlver at the National Archives. Thus, there is a strong possibility that McAlver did not serve in the USCT.
