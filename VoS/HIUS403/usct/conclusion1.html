Today, the United States Colored Troops (USCT) are finally receiving the recognition they deserve as significant members of the Union Army. Scholars have only recently started to define the USCT's place in history and the movie Glory even brought their story into popular culture. In 1997, the United States government comissioned the African-American Civil War Memorial. It will be unveiled in March 1998 and is the first "official" USCT war memorial.

The purpose of this site is to give human faces and names to the saga of African-American troops in the Civil War. This site is unique in that it focuses on the colored troops from a single county in Pennsylvania. These men all came from Franklin County, yet they fought for their freedom across the country. While we certainly hope that you have gained a greater understanding of each of these men's lives, we also hope that you have learned a great deal about the United States Colored Troops in general and their important role in the Civil War.
