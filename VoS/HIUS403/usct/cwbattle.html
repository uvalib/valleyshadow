CIVIL WAR BATTLES
UNITED STATES COLORED TROOPS

Researched and Compiled by Bennie J. McRae, Jr. lwf@coax.net

NOTE: Listed below are regimental designations as of the end of the Civil War. Many regiments when organized were assigned State or Corps d' Afrique designations and redesignated after the establishment of the Bureau of Colored Troops on May 22, 1863 under General Order No. 143 by order of the Secretary of War. For example, the 3rd U.S. Colored Cavalry Regiment was organized as the 1st Mississippi Cavalry Regiment (African Descent); the 79th U.S. Infantry Regiment, organized as 1st Kan sas Colored Volunteers; the 78th U.S. Colored Infantry Regiment, organized as the 6th Infantry Corps d' Afrique; the 5th U.S. Colored Infantry Regiment, organized as the 127th Ohio Infantry (African Descent), etc.

Another example, during the Battle of Milliken's Bend, Louisiana the participating regiments were designated as follows; 9th Louisiana Volunteers (5th U.S. Colored Heavy Artillery), 11th Louisiana Volunteers (49th U.S. Colored Infantry), and 1st Missis sippi Volunteers (51st U.S. Colored Infantry).

A number of regiments not listed below participated in minor skrimishes or performed mostly picket or other non-combat duties, therefore show no record of participation in any major battle or skirmish.

Four regiments maintained State designations throughout the war: 54th and 55th Massachusetts Volunteers, 5th Massachusetts Colored Cavalry, and the 29th Connecticut Infantry.


ABBREVIATIONS

  • BTRY = Unit of Light Artillery
  • CONN INF = Connecticut Volunteer Infantry
  • MASS INF = Massachusetts Volunteer Infantry
  • USCC = United States Colored Cavalry Regiment
  • USCHA = United States Colored Heavy Artillery Regiment
  • USCI = United States Colored Infantry Regiment
  • USCLA = United States Colored Light Artillery Regiment
