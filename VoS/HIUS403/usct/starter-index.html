HIST 403: Digital History of the American Civil War

USCT Team Starter Index

The USCT Project will trace the history of the Franklin County men who fought in these units. The USCT were not organized by county. So, Franklin men fought in a wide number of units. Some fought, for example, in the 54th Massachusetts, made famous for its glorious charge on Fort Wagner and for the death of its Colonel, Robert H. Shaw. Others fought in USCT designated units. Reconstructing the histories of these Franklin people will require a good deal of historical detective work.

The first order of business for this team will be to locate the units in which these men fought and then going to National Archives to retreive their Compiled Service Records for entry into a database. The team will have a list of all Franklin black and mulatto men of military age and a web site to check their unit designations. After compliling the list of men who served and their units, the team will go to National Archives for the CSR.
