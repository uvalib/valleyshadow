Valley Spirit, April 20, 1859, p. 5, c. 4

A Good Idea

It is the intention, we understand, in future, to devote our entire term of Court for the special purpose of trying the filthy cases among the colored population of our country. This will afford them time to gather together as many cases as will occupy the Court and time, and also to crowd our pretty Court House with a crowd of drunken, worthless darkies, who can ornament the building by poking their heads out of the windows, standing around in every hole and corner, and squirting toacco juice on the passers by. While this arrangement is being made it is also intended to give notice to the public that J.L. Dechert has the best, cheapest and prettiest stock of Hats, Caps, &c, this side of Philadelphia. Just give him a call and see.
