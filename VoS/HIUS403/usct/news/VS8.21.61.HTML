Court Proceedings

Commonwealth vs George Hackerdorn--Murder. Defendant was charged with being one of the parties who participated in the riotous affray, which resulted in the murder of Frank Jones, on the evening of the first of June last, on "Federal Hill," near this town. The principal point in this case was the identity of the prisoner. It being urged by the defence that the witness were not acquainted with any of the parties to the affair, and that the military shirt worn by prisoner was the same kind as all the soldiers in town wore. The commonwealth failing in this the jury after remaining out a few hours returned a verdict of not Guilty. Eyster & Bonebrake for Com.; Reilly & Sharpe for def't.
