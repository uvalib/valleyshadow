Lincoln issued the Emancipation Proclamation, freeing slaves in AR, AL, FL, GA, MS, NC, SC, TX, VA, & LA. Confederates captured Galveston, TX.

Mid 01-1863 Union Brethren Church held a series of revivals.
01-20-1863 Spectator reported only 49 marriage licenses issued in Augusta in 1862, far fewer than in 1861. During 1862, 1102 people imprisoned for misdemeanors; 49 for felonies; 25 for running away from their masters; 2 for kidnapping; and 2 for murder.
01-21-1863 Lt. E. N. Ford of the Provost Guard in Chambersburg was shot & killed in an attempt to arrest a deserter in Fulton County.
01-26-1863 Capt. S. R. McKesson & the noncommissioned officers for recruiting business left Chambersburg to rejoint the 77th PA.
01-27-1867 A German family resisted a fire co.'s attempt to take water from a nearby cistern to save Nicolas Uglo's stable.

02-02-1863 House of Representatives passed a bill providing for the employment of black soldiers.
02-03-1863 Spectator complained Gen. Jones did not do enough to protect the people of the lower Valley, despite the fact that he had more men & artillery than Gen. Milroy. Richmond Whig raised questions about Col. M. G. Harman's alleged unethical conduct as quartermaster. Department of the East created.
02-04-1863 Confederate Gen. Magruder declared Port Galveston, TX open to the commerce of the world.
02-09-1863 Chambersburg Female Seminary began its spring session.
02-11-1863 Confederates attempted to assassinate Gen. Banks in New Orleans, LA.
02-12-1863 The Wesley Church, a black congregation near Chambersburg, sponsored 2 days of public entertainment. US Senate passed the National Currency Bill. Jefferson Davis suggested the Confederate Congress adopt retaliatory measures against the operation of the Emancipation Proclamation.
Mid 02-1863 Capt. S. Brownson of Mercersburg promoted to Maj. of Company C, 126th PA.
02-16-1863 US Senate passed the Conscription Bill.
02-18-1863 4 Chambersburg men died either from wounds or illness received thus far in the war.
02-20-1863 Vindicator resumed publication. Col. Harman appealed to Augusta for socks and information regarding members of the regiment who were AWOL. Lt. William Burgess of the 6th PA released on parole from a Confederate prison in Richmond & allowed to visit his Franklin family. National Currency Bill passed the US House of Representatives.
02-21-1863 Gen. Fitz-John Porter dismissed from U.S. service having been adjudged under the 9th & 52nd Articles of War.
02-24-1863 Territory of AZ formed from the Territory of NM.
02-25-1863 Congress passed the National Banking Act, providing for a system of national banks. National Currency law secured by U.S. bonds passed and approved.New Exemption Bill passed by the Confederate Senate.
02-26-1863 Mr. Foote offered peace resolutions in the Confederate Congress.
02-27-1863 Vindicator printed discussions of whether the counties of northwestern VA will remain part of VA & the Confederacy.
02-28-1863 Confederate war steamer Nashville destroyed in the Ogeechee River, GA, by the Montauk.
Early in 03-1863 Atlantic & OH Telegraph Co. began construction of a 2nd line between Chambersburg & Harrisburg.
03-01-1863 Franklin received 12 to 14 inches of snow.
03-02-1863 Congress authorized a railway gauge of 4 feet 8.5 inches for the track width of the Union Pacific Railroad.
03-03-1863 Spectator complained that the Confederate military had been impressing food supplies at prices greatly below market value. The War Department informed all men who are liable to conscription that if they come forward & volunteer for service, they are eligible for a $50 bounty & furlough. Territory of ID created. First US Conspriction Act in the nation's history passed. Congress initiated free delivery of mail in cities. Congress resolved to suppress the rebellion, rather than attempt mediation. Congress authorized a $900,000,000 loan for 1863-1865.
03-06-1863 Vindicator described a meeting at the Wheeling courthouse of persons opposed to establishing a new state & recounted the response to the meeting as published in a pro-new state newspaper, Intelligencer. Gen. Hunter ordered the drafting of blacks for federal service in the Department of the South.
03-10-1863 A concert at the VA Female Institute netted $325 for the relief of Fredericksburg. A concert by the Staunton 5th VA Regiment band raised $400 for the soldiers' families. Spectator implored farmers to plant corn & "renounce cotton & tobacco."
03-14-1863 Democrats of Green Township met to select candidates for the township election.
03-17-1863 Engagement at Rappahannock Station & Kelly's Ford, VA.
03-18-1863 South Ward Democrats met to select a ticket for the spring election.
03-21-1863 Lurgan School District Teacher's Institute met in the Roxbury schoolroom.
03-25-1863 Impressments of private property, including slaves, in the Confederate States authorized.
03-27-1863 President Davis proclaimed March 27th as a day of fasting, humiliation, & prayer.
03-28-1863 A valuable horse belonging to John Funk stolen from the shed in Kurtz's yard. A new Provost Guard arrived in Chambersburg. The first African-American regiment from the northern states left Boston, MA.
Late 03-1863 Three Franklin men who were taken as political prisoners during the raid of Stewart's Cavalry were released from Libby Prison in Richmond, VA.
Early in 04-1863 John Forney put on trial for the murder of Lt. Ford of the Provost Guard.
04-02-1863 Spectator reported that a band of robbers attempted to rob Richmond stores in broad daylight, but denied that the riot stemmed from want.
04-03-1863 Howe Y. Peyton announced his candidacy for the House of Delegates. Vindicator published an editorial complaining of the general filth of Chambersburg. Bread riots in Richmond.
04-04-1863 4 slaves ran away from William J. Shumate. Public meeting produced a petition asking the legislature to pass a law forcing the railroad co. to bridge the Waynesboro Cut.
04-07-1863 Spectator complained that Southerners were preying upon each other. Confederates evacuated Island Number 10 in the Mississippi River.
04-11-1863 Member of the 25th & 31st VA Regiment became drunk & rowdy as they passed through Staunton, & were subsequently placed in the guardhouse. Richard Simpson, a bound boy belonging to Adam Piper, ran away from Concord Township.
04-13-1863 Presbyterian Sabbath School held an anniversary. Democratic county committee met & selected a delegate to the Democratic state convention. Gen. Burnside ordered the death penalty for aiding Confederates.
04-14-1863 William Bullock of PA patented a continuous-roll printing press.
Mid 04-1863 Edward McPherson of Franklin was nominated Deputy Commissioner of Internal Revenue.
04-17-1863 Grierson's Raid began.
04-22-1863 Sanford's Ethiopian Opera Troupe performed at Franklin Hall.
04-23-1863 Union troops raided the Valley and came as far south as Woodstock.
04-24-1863 Governor Letcher announced his candidacy for representative from his old district in the next Congress.
04-25-1863 Meeting held at the courthouse to establish a Democratic club opposed to the party in power. Series of altercations occurred between Chambersburg residents & members of the Provost Guard.
04-26-1863 33 new communicants received by the Lutheran Church of Chambersburg.
04-27-1863 40 to 50 black recruits left for MA to join a new regiment being formed there.
4-30-1863 Franklin residents observed the national day of humiliation, fasting, & prayer. Day of fasting in the U.S.
Late in 04-1863 Bill legalizing the payment of bounties to volunteers passed both houses. PA Senate voted to reduce the pay of the state militia.
Early in 05-1863 Ladies Aid Society of Chambersburg became an official auxiliary to the Philadelphia agency of the U.S. Sanitary Commission.
05-01-1863 Battle of Chancellorsville, in Spotsylvania County, VA began: Gen. Lee defeated Gen. Hooker. Battle of Port Gibson, MS.
05-02-1863 9 members of the 5th VA Infantry killed & 111 wounded during the Battle of Chancellorsville. 81 members of the 126th PA reported killed or wounded during the Battle of Chancellorsville. Chambersburg Academy students gave a concert in Franklin Hall. Democrats of Washington Township met to establish a Democratic club. Gen. Jackson & his men mistaken for Union soldiers & fired on by their own forces. Gen. Jackson shot in the left arm.
05-03-1863 2nd Battle of Fredericksburg, VA: Gen. Sedgewick & the Union forces defeated Gen. Lee & the Confederate forces. Confederates abandoned Grand Gulf, below Vicksburg, MS.
05-04-1863 Chambersburg borough elections held. Chambersburg Academy students gave a 2nd concert in Franklin Hall. By orders of Gen. Burnside, Clement L. Vallandingham arrested at Dayton, OH for alleged treasonable utterances.
05-05-1863 Vindicator supported Baldwin, the former Whig. Spectator supported Letcher, the former Democrat.
05-10-1863 Gen. Jackson died.
05-12-1863 Spectator questioned Col. Harman for giving his wife a silver set that had been captured. Franklin Farmers & Mechanics Association met to elect officers. Battle of Raymond, MS.
05-14-1863 Members of the 126th PA arrived back in Franklin. Gen. Grant occupied Jackson, MS.
05-15-1863 Corbin & Gran hanged at Sandusky for recruiting within the Union lines.
Mid 05-1863 David Lawson of Montgomery Township arrested for treason & sent over the lines. Frederick Douglass gave a speech in Philadelphia calling for blacks to be accepted into the "American family."
05-16-1863 3 slaves ran away from their owner's home on the South River near Stuart's Draft. Battle of Champion Hills, MS.
05-17-1863 Battle of Big Black River, MS.
05-18-1863 Confederates retired as the siege began within the defenses at Vicksburg, MS.
05-19-1863 Two prisoners broke out of the county jail.
05-20-1863 Teachers' Association of Franklin held its 7th semi-annual convention.
05-21-1863 Col. Harman takes exception to the Spectator's comments about the silver set & beats Mr. Mauzy, the Spectator's editor, senseless. U.S. forces began an assault on the Vicksburg works.
05-22-1863 Vindicator called for women of Augusta to return to the Staunton hospital to treat wounded soldiers.
05-23-1863 Stockholders of the gas co. met to determine what could be done about the lack of gas in Staunton.
05-25-1863 33 members of the Union League paraded through Chambersburg.
05-27-1863 Valley Spirit editors called for Democrats to organize against the Loyal Leagues being formed in the county. Gen. Banks unsuccessfully assaulted Confederate works at Port Hudson.
05-28-1863 1st Northern African-American regiment, the 44th MA, departed for Hilton Head, SC.
05-29-1863 Gen. Burnside ordered the suppression of the Chicago Times because of its violently anti-Lincoln & pro-Southern opinions.
05-30-1863 Democrats of Chambersburg & the surrounding area met in Franklin Hall.
05-31-1863 German Reformed Church of Chambersburg observed the 300th anniversary of the Heidelberg Catechism.
Early in 06-1863 John Baldwin won reelection to the Confederate Congress. Mass meeting in Philadelphia protested the arrest of C. L. Vallandingham for treason.
06-01-1863 Augusta citizens created a Central Committee to devise measures for the relief of the exiles from western VA. J. F. Johnson sold three slaves in Staunton. Lincoln revoked Gen. Burside's order given 3 days earlier.
06-02-1863 The Central Committee appointed a committee of ladies to visit exiled families from western VA as they arrived in Staunton to ascertain the needs of the refugees.
06-03-1863 Valley Spirit editors complained about the frequent loud gatherings of blacks near Wallace's Corner in Chambersburg. Gen. Lee began movement for the invasion of the North.
06-08-1863 Col. McClure & H. S. Stoner purchased the Franklin Repository & Transcript. Cavalry battle at Beverly's Ford, VA. Departments of Monongahela & Susquehanna created.
06-12-1863 Christian Commission met at the Lutheran Church.
06-14-1863 Battle of Winchester, VA began.

06-17-1863 Democratic state convention in Harrisburg opened & nominated George W. Woodward for governor.
06-19-1863 Gen. Stuart's cavalry began to fight screening action along modern Route 50. Confederates invaded IN.
06-20-1863 WV admitted to the Union as the 35th state.
06-22-1863 Augusta residents held a public meeting to establish a home defense. Confederate troops occupied Chambersburg.
06-24-1863 Maj. Harman demanded that residents of Chambersburg provide invading Confederate troops with clothing & supplies. Confederate troops began to cross the Potomac River. Gen. Rosecrans advanced from Murfreesboro, TN against Gen. Bragg's forces at Tullahoma, TN.
06-26-1863 Army of the Potomac crossed the Potomac River.
06-27-1863 Confederate troops under Gen. Lee began the invasion of southern PA. Confederates advanced within a few miles of Harrisburg, PA.
06-30-1863 Augusta Female Seminary sponsored a concert & gave the proceeds to benefit sick & wounded soldiers. Battle of Hanover Junction, PA.
Late in 06-1863 During the occupation, Confederates broke into the Columbus Lodge of Odd Fellows & cut to pieces the regalia there.
Early in 07-1863 Dinner given in honor of the returned soldiers in Waynesboro. A number of Chambersburg citizens arrested by Confederates on the suspicion of spying.
07-01-1863 1st day of the Battle of Gettysburg, PA.
07-02-1863 Yankee prisoners taken at Upperville & Winchester arrived in Staunton. 2nd day of the Battle of Gettysburg, PA.
07-03-1863 The 5th VA Infantry & the 52nd VA Infantry suffered heavy losses during the Battle of Gettysburg. Occupying rebel forces retreated from Chambersburg. 3rd day of the Battle of Gettysburg, PA: Pickett's Charge; Gen. Meade & defeated Gen. Lee.
07-04-1863 Chambersburg residents celebrated the 4th of July by raising a flag on a makeshift flagpole. Battle of Vicksburg, MS: Confederates under Gen. Pemberton surrendered to Gen. Grant. Battle of Helena, AR.
07-05-1863 Absalom Shetter,a farmer just outside Chambersburg, hung himself after Confederates made off with his stock & grain.
07-07-1863 Confederate raiders under Gen. Morgan crossed the OH River at Brandenburg, KY into IN.
07-08-1863 Valley Spirit & Franklin Repository resumed publication. Battle of Port Hudson, MS.
07-09-1863 20 prisoners who had been captured by Col. Jackson at Beverly arrived in Staunton. A riot broke out between members of the NY militia sent to defend Chambersburg & other Union troops stationed around Chambersburg.
07-10-1863 Franklin held official 4th of July exercises. Maj. Couch arrived in Chambersburg to form an army corps for the defense of PA. Battle of Ft. Wagner, SC began.
07-11-1863 Telegraph line between Chambersburg & Loudon reconstructed. Conscription under the draft began in New York City.
07-13-1863 Governor Curtin visited Chambersburg. Anti-draft riots broke out in New York City for 3 days, with nearly 1000 killed or wounded before federal troops restored order.
Mid 07-1863 Governor Curtin called for 840 more troops from Franklin. PA militiaman stabbed by a drunk soldier in Chambersburg. Franklin unable to pay all the taxes as a result of the rebel occupation
07-15-1863 Mr. Kyle & his son killed when they attempted to chisel open a shell they found. 2nd Battle of Jackson, MS.
07-17-1863 Col. Davidson notified Staunton that soldiers who beg for food are "skulkers" & not legitimately absent from their commands.
07-18-1863 Gabriel Hirsh's store burglarized. U.S. troops repulsed in their assault on Ft. Wagner, Morris Island, SC.
07-20-1863 Chambersburg Bank resumed business after hiding its assets during the Confederate raid.
07-26-1863 Gen. Morgan's raid in IN & OH ended.
07-28-1863 Confederate officers impressed horses in Staunton.
07-30-1863 Lincoln issued a proclamation for the protection of African-American troops against retaliation by the Confederates.
Late in 07-1863 George Platt of the 126th PA returned to resume his dentistry practice in Chambersburg.
08-01-1863 Union League met to organize a reception for the 158th PA. Jefferson Davis granted a general pardon to all AWOL Confederate soldiers, as long as they return to their posts by August 20th.
08-06-1863 Franklin residents observed the National Day of Thanksgiving & Prayer. US day of National Thanksgiving observed.
08-08-1863 County school superintendent began examining applicants for teaching positions.
08-12-1863 Gen. Toombs exposed the bankruptcy of the Confederacy.
Mid 08-1863 J. K. Reid collected clothing for his nephew & other Franklin citizens in Libby Prison in Richmond, VA.

08-19-1863 Horses reported stolen by thieves disguised as Union soldiers.
08-21-1863 Jefferson Davis proclaimed the 21st a day of fasting, humiliation, & prayer.
08-23-1853 Union troops crossed the mountains at Buffalo Gap, & Confederate forces & local citizens readied themselves for an attack.
08-25-1863 Spectator, citing Nostradamus, predicted the world would end in 1866. Col. Baldwin appealed to Augustans to form volunteer companies for home defense.
09-01-1863 Samuel Fisher elected president of the county committee at the County Democratic Convention.

09-19-1863 Battle of Chickamauga, TN began.

09-29-1863 State agricultural fair opened in Montgomery County, PA.
10-02-1863 State agricultural fair ended.
10-03-1863 Board of Enrollment of the 16th Congressional District of PA exempted 12 Franklin men from military service. Lincoln proclaimed Thanksgiving Day a national holiday.
10-14-1863 Battle of Bristow Station, VA.
10-15-1863 Methodist Episcopal Sabbath School gave an exhibition for the benefit of poor children in Staunton.
10-17-1863 Lincoln called for 300,000 men for 3 years.
10-18-1863 Constable Unger shot by John Flohr of Waynesboro while he tried to break up a riot in the schoolhouse yard.
10-20-1863 Spectator reported that some farmers were skirting the price regulations.
10-21-1863 Repository reported that all 3 members of the John Brown family died of a fever over the last month.

10-28-1863 Reports surfaced of Franklin men trying to evade the draft by presenting false exemption certificates.
10-29-1863 Capt. Chambers, formerly of Harper's Ferry, shot & beat Capt. Sutton of Richmond, after the 2 quarreled in front of the VA Hotel.
Late in 10-1863 George Yates of Mt. Sidney & a number of citizens of Rockingham County arrested on charges of helping deserters from the Confederate armies to the Yankee lines.
Early in 11-1863 Franklin citizens moved from Libby Prison in Richmond, VA to a better prison in Salisbury, NC.
11-01-1863 12 new members joined the Methodist Episcopal Church in Staunton during the religious revival that began amongst the soldiers in the hospital. A plot to liberate Confederate prisoners in OH discovered.
11-06-1863 Near Greenbrier, a greatly outnumbered Confederate force under Gen. Echols & Col. Jackson fought heartily, but retreated in the face of a determined attack led by Gen. Kelly & Gen. Averill. Vindicator published an excerpt from Gen. Lee's report on Gen. Imboden's latest military feat: surrounding Charlestown & capturing most of the Yankee force there.
11-07-1863 Union troops under the command of Gen. Averill began to move towards Staunton. John Birny, an Irish teamster & Chambersburg resident, fatally kicked in the stomach by a horse.
11-09-1863 In the trial of the 10 men charged with helping deserters, the men were released from prison & re-arrested & transported to Staunton for trial.
11-11-1863 Franklin Teachers' Association opened meetings.

11-13-1863 Home Guard units in Staunton prepared to march to the mountains to meet Gen. Averill's troops.

11-16-1863 Jacob McCune of Mercersburg died of paralysis. Battle of Campbell's Station, TN.
11-18-1863 2 NJ companies stationed in Chambersburg.

11-29-1863 Battle of Ft. Sanders, TN.

12-07-1863 Hope Fire Co. held elections. New County officers took their posts.

12-26-1863 Capt. Schelling's Company D, 16th PA Invalid Corps left Chambersburg for Elmira, NY.
12-31-1863 Children of the Presbyterian Sunday School treated to a festival.
