Dates Augusta County Franklin County Virginia Pennsylvania National
01-1867 General Assembly passed a bill saying, no contract between a white & a black, for the labor of service of the latter for more than 2 months, shall be binding on the black unless the contract is written & signed by both parties & a public official.
01-05-1867 Johnson vetoed the Suffrage in the District of Columbia Bill
01-07-1867 Representative James Ashley of Ohio charged Johnson with usurpation, corruption & interference in election. The case was referred to the Judiciary Committee.
01-08-1867 New directors of the 1st National Bank of Greencastle elected. Overriding Johnson's veto, Congress granted suffrage to African-Americans in Washington, D.C.
01-09-1867 Valley Spirit reported that a faith doctor visited Waynesboro. Legislature rejected the proposed constitutional amendment.
01-13-1867 John Geary inaugurated as governor. Simon Cameron inaugurated as U. S. senator.
01-16-1867 Valley Spirit reported counterfeits of the Nation Bank's $5 bills surfaced, & that Reverend E. E. Higbee will be the new professor of church history at the Theological Seminary of Mercersburg.
01-17-1867 Fire destroyed Jacob Middour's residence.
01-19-1867 Young men of the county founded the Philomathesian Society, a debating club.
01-22-1867 Staunton Musical Association formed. Counterfeit $5 National notes surfaced. Spectator reported that the African-American population in VA declined by over 190,000 since 02-01-1860.
01-25-1867 Reverend S. D. Stuart delivered a lecture at the Staunton Lyceum detailing his trip to England.
01-29-1867 Friendship Fire Co. quickly extinguished a fire at John A. Grove's marble works.
02-01-1867 A. H. H. Stuart left Staunton for Richmond to settle difficulties between VA & WV.
02-02-1867 Billy Wilson, a freedman, charged with burning a barn, claimed another black paid him to do so. Valley Spirit reported new counterfeits surfaced.
02-03-1867 William & Henry Bird, two freedmen, charged with burning a house. Legislature debated whether or not to allow blacks to ride in public conveyances.
02-07-1867 The Chambersburg Cornet Band conducted a play with a scene involving a white man dressed as a black woman.
02-08-1867 Dr. C. R. Harris delivered a lecture on "Freedom of Thought" at the Staunton Lyceum.
02-11-1867 The local Building Association formed.
02-12-1867 Martin Snyder arrested for the rape of Mary Elizabeth Eberly, a 10-year-old resident of the local Children's Home. Legislature ratified the 14th Amendment.
02-13-1867 Valley Spirit reported a fire broke out in Joseph McKinney's house.
02-15-1867 Concert held at the Presbyterian Church.
02-20-1867 Valley Spirit reported Pope Wilson & George Book, 2 freedmen, arrested for stealing a coat from White's clothing store.
02-21-1867 Concert at the Presbyterian Church repeated.
02-25-1867 Congressional resolution provided for a survey for a canal at Darien, Panama.
02-27-1867 Valley Spirit reported the temperance fair last week raised nearly $2000.
03-1867 Episcopal Methodist Church made appointments. Senate called for a convention under the provisions of the military force bill. House of Delegates passed a bill forbidding the sale or distribution of liquor near voting places.
03-01-1867 NE admitted as the 37th state.
03-03-1867 Thieves broke into Mr. Greathead's store & stole money & merchandise. Under 1st Reconstruction Act, VA became part of Military District 1. 1st Reconstruction Act, which denied removal powers of the president, passed. Tenure of Civil Office Bill passed over Johnson's veto. National Bankruptcy Bill passed. Department of Education established. Judiciary Committee concerning impeachment recommended a continuance of investigation.
03-04-1867 Sherry's Theatrical Troupe performed at Repository Hall.
03-05-1867 Voting rights of blacks challenged in Alexandria, when 1000 black votes were rejected.
03-07-1867 John Randolph, a freedman, imprisoned for stealing shoes from P. B. Hoge's store. Order of the Knights of St. Crispin established to halt the rise of unemployment in the shoe industry.
03-11-1867 Johnson began assigning generals to the command of the 5 military districts in the South.
03-12-1867 Reported that a Radical paper will soon be started in Staunton.
03-13-1867 Valley Spirit reported on the projected railroad from Carlisle to Pine Grove in Franklin County. Fire destroyed a steam sawmill of the Doubling Gap Lumber & Mining Co. & Matthew Elder's barn. Gen. Schofield arrived in Richmond & replaced Governor Pierpont with Gen. Wells. Trustees of the Agricultural College of PA met.
03-15-1867 Election for borough, township, and election officers held.
03-16-1867 Telegraph office opened in Waynesboro at J. F. Kurtz's Drug Store.
03-18-1867 George Rock stabbed Jacob Fisher 11 times during a scuffle in Quincy.
03-20-1867 North Ward Democrats met at Montgomery House. South Ward Democrats met at William Bender's House. Valley Spirit announced Governor Greary pardoned Mrs. Bernthisel of Greencastle of her adultery conviction.
03-23-1867 2nd Reconstruction Act, which provided for registration of all qualified votes, passed over Johnson's veto.
03-25-1867 Ladies' Memorial Association hosted a fair & dinner to raise money for the soldiers' cemetery.
03-30-1867 Secretary of State Seward agreed to pay Russia $7,200,000 for Alaska.
03-31-1867 A thwarted burglary occurred at George Etter's home.
04-1867 Frank Wilson of Staunton elected the "Smallest Man" of the Ugly Club at the University of Virginia. E. L. Houff, a delegate from Augusta to the Loyalist Convention returned home unhappy with the proceedings. William Marshall charged with the rape of Elizabeth White. Explosion in the coal mines outside Richmond killed about 70 men. Loyalist Convention held in Richmond. Conflicts arose over whether or not African-Americans should be allowed to ride in Richmond streetcars.
04-01-1867 Col. McClure & his family departed for Montana to inspect gold & silver mines, in which he had a stake.
04-02-1867 Jacob Foreman's son drowned while playing in the Conococheague.
04-08-1867 Col. Bolivar Christian lectured at the Staunton Lyceum on the character of the people of the Valley of VA.
04-09-1867 The home of Samuel Parent burned. John McCoy of Mifflin Township committed suicide. Congress ratified the purchase of Alaska.
04-15-1867 The Freedmen of Staunton held a meeting and produced resolutions.
04-17-1867 Valley Spirit reported that an act passed the state legislature incorporating a co. in Chambersburg for the purpose of erecting a Market House & a Public Hall.
04-20-1867 The remains of an infant were found buried on a lot near Burke's Foundry. Schuyler Colfax delivered his lecture, "Across the Continent" in Repository Hall.
04-21-1867 Franklin County Bible Society held its anniversary meeting in the German Reformed Church.
04-24-1867 Valley Spirit reported Matthew P. Walsh was confirmed as the new Postmaster of Chambersburg.
04-29-1867 Lightning struck Jacob Lear's blacksmith shop in Shady Grove.

05-02-1867 Chambersburg Musical Union concert.
05-04-1867 Reverend George D. Chenowith, corresponding secretary of the State Temperance Union, spoke at the Methodist Episcopalian Church.
05-06-1867 H. M. Sibbett won the post of Chief Burgess in the Waynesboro election.
05-10-1867 2nd Annual Floral Tribute to the memory of the soldiers buried in the Soldiers' Cemetery. 4th anniversary of Stonewall Jackson's death celebrated
05-11-1867 Jefferson Davis visited Richmond.
05-13-1867 Jefferson Davis taken to Richmond on habeas corpus and admitted to bail.

06-04-1867 Hope Fire Co. held elections
06-05-1867 Valley Spirit reported the town council approved a plan to number dwellings and businesses in Chambersburg.
06-07-1867 Peter McGaffigan drowned in Falling Spring.
