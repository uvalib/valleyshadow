• Michael G. Harman, Recruiting Broadside, May 30, 1861
    In this broadside from American Memory website of the Library of Congress, Michael G. Harman called upon the "men of Virginia" to defend "our homes and firesides--the honor of our wives and daughters." Harman proposed Patrick Henry's words, "Give me Liberty or give me Death," as their rallying motto.
  • Michael G. Harman, Broadside, 1861
    In this broadside from American Memory website of the Library of Congress, Michael G. Harman called upon the "men of Virginia" drive back their "Abolition foes."
  • Michael G. Harman, Notice to the 52nd Regiment, June 22, 1862
    In this notice to members of the 52nd Regiment of Virginia Volunteers, Michael G. Harman listed soldiers who were absent without leave. Harman promised that AWOL soldiers who reported to him would be allowed to rejoin their regiment without punishment. This broadside is from the American Memory website of the Library of Congress.
