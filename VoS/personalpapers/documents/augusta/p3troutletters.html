• Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, October 2, 1868
    Nicholas K. Trout questions Alfred Chapman on several claims. He also requests that Chapman make several purchase on his behalf.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, [?] 1868
    Nicholas K. Trout writes a friend about legal matters and busy life.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, January 4, 1869
    Nicholas K. Trout asks Alfred Chapman about a woman's claim. He also inquires about Chapman's Christmas and New Year.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, March 2, 1869
    Nicholas K. Trout write Alfred Chapman requesting help for a widow and personal trouble he having with the state legislature.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, March 5, 1869
    Nicholas K. Trout writes about the troubles he is experiencing in attempting to be restored or reinstated to the Union. His nomination is being blocked for personal and political reasons.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, March 28, 1869
    Nicholas K. Trout discusses stocks, dividends, and debts with Alfred Chapman.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, March 30, 1869
    Nicholas K. Trout provides Alfred Chapman with the details of a particular case and comments upon Judge Sheffey and Judge Fultz. He also asks Chapman about the claim of a soldier's widow.
  • Nicholas K. Trout to Alfred Chapman, April 2, 1869
    Nicholas K. Trout writes Alfred Chapman about an important meeting in Washington that he will have to miss because of arthritis and a previous commitment to attend a hearing.
