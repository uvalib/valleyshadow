• Peter Eidson Wilson to Henry H. Hamilton, January 5, 1863
    Wilson talks about his experience sitting on the general courts martial and writing the letters for his company.
  • Joseph Alfred Wilson to Henry H. Hamilton, January 11, 1863
    Joseph writes to Henry telling him that he is thinking of deserting. He also mentions his homesickness and the fact that a few officers have taken ill.
  • Joseph Alfred Wilson to Bell Wilson, January 21, 1863
    Joseph writes to his sister about family, his military company, and the weather.
  • Joseph Alfred Wilson to Henry H. Hamilton, February 17, 1863
    Joseph writes to his family friend Henry with instructions on sending him stamps. He also tells about new military promotions.
  • Joseph Alfred Wilson to Henry H. Hamilton, February 20, 1863
    Joseph writes to Henry Hamilton about his regiment's problems with spoiled meat and complains that his sister's letters are too short.
