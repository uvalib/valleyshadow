• Mary Pastor to John Cochran, Sr., July 6, 1859
    Pastor writes of a gathering of Virginia companies for the Fourth of July celebration and exchanges updates about family, friends, and acquaintances.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, October 8, 1860
    Cochran outlines the political argument supporting southern secession.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, December 11, 1860
    Talks about the growing anxiety and uncertainty surrounding South Carolina's threat of secession and evaluates the South's readiness for a war.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, December 21, 1860
    Cochran reflects on the meaning of South Carolina's secession.
  • A. G. Guskins to His Cousin, December 30, 1860
    Guskins writes from South Carolina and provides an update on political life in that state following its secession from the Union.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, January 12, 1861
    Cochran shares his thoughts on Virginia's secession deliberations.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, February 14, 1861
    Predicts that the war with the North will begin in less than sixty days.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, March 3, 1861
    Cochran talks about fomenting a revolution in the state if Virginia opts for peace over secession.
  • John H. Cochran to His Mother, March 19, 1861
    Predicts that the eastern part of Virginia will "revolt" against the western portion over the question of secession.
