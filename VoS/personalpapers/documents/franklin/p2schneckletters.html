• Benjamin S. Schneck to Margaretta S. Keller, September 12, 1862
    Schneck writes to his sister, Margaretta, about his trip west through Ohio, and how he was forced to turn back after rumors of an imminent Confederate march into Pennsylvania.
  • Benjamin S. Schneck to Margaretta S. Keller, September 24, 1862
    Schneck's letter describes the wounded soldiers of a battle, and asks how to get in touch with his sister's son, Daniel.
  • Benjamin S. Schneck to Margaretta S. Keller, October 13, 1862
    Schneck writes his sister about a Confederate raid on their horses and supply depot, as well as a false alarm that almost sent many civilians up in arms against their own Union forces.
  • Benjamin S. Schneck to Margaretta S. Keller and Her Husband, August 3, 1864
    Schneck's letter describes the Confederate invasion and burning of Chambersburg of late-July 1864.
