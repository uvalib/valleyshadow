• Augustus Hover to His Mother, April 20, 1863
    Hover tells his mother about his marching orders and about how he has to carry eight days of rations. He also mentions a portrait of himself and says that he is sending money home.
  • Augustus Hover to His Mother, June 15, 1864
    Hover talks about re-enlistment, efforts to build breastworks between the James and Rapidan Rivers, and an apparent scarcity of writing instruments.
  • Augustus Hover to His Mother, August [1864?]
    Hover asks his mother to send him a box of supplies.
  • Augustus Hover to His Mother, November 2, 1864
    Hover mentions his fear of the draft and asks about his family and friends.
