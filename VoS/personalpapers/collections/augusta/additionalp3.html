  • William F. Brand, 1865-1867

    These letter primarily between Brand and his future wife Amanda Armentrout, concern the later part of their courtship. Armentrout frequently mentions her concern over Brand's religious views and temperance.

  • John D. Imboden, 1865-1881

    These letters between Imboden and Annie Lockett, his future wife, concern their relationship and the difficulties of life in postwar Virginia.

  • John W. McCue to Colonel & Mrs. Sepps, September 1866

    McCue's description of his capture, imprisonment, and trial at the culmination of the war.

  • Henry Ott

    These three letters primarily concern prices of goods in postwar shops.

  • Alexander H. H. Stuart, 1867-1873

    This collection of letters to Alexander H. H. Stuart concern Reconstruction-era politics.
