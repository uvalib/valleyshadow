Abraham Essick

Abraham Essick was a native of Franklin County but left that area to become a Lutheran minister in the 1840s. His diary begins when he received his license to preach in 1849. It continues through the 1850s, when Essick preached before congregations in Pennsylvania, Ohio, and Maryland. Of special interest is his account of his time in Winchester, Virginia, where he also preached, and where he took note of the differences between Virginia and Pennsylvania. Essick returned to Franklin County in 1861, and his diary continues with his thoughts on the challenges of preaching in wartime.
