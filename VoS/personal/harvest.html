In conjunction with our collaboration with the Woodrow Wilson Birthplace Museum in Staunton, we held a "History Harvest" in October 1994. Members of the community brought family memorabilia, which we digitized, and are now available in the Archive.
Donation information is available.


Letters (Excluding those elsewhere in the archive)

  • Letter to commissioners R. G. Bickle, J. B. Evans, and W. B. Kayser from Jedediah Hotchkiss, No Date: Page 1, Page 2.
    Hotchkiss writes about macadamizing Main Street in Staunton.
  • Letter from Llewellyn Merriwether Humphris to Merriwether Alan Humphris, March 19, 1862
    Llewellyn writes to Merriwether about avoiding Confederate service.
  • Harman family letters
    Two letters from Col. William H. Harman to Col. Asher Harman.

Business and Financial Documents

  • The Downey Family papers
    These papers consist primarily of receipts, accounts, and wills. Daniel Downey was an Internal Revenue Storekeeper in Augusta during the 1870s.
  • James Poage's Business papers
    These papers are a collection of receipts, logbooks, and other business documents.
  • $100 Note to the Augusta County Fair, signed by S. L. Timberlake, April 10, 1868.

    • Back of Note

  • Three pieces of 25 cent Augusta County script, May 25, 1862
