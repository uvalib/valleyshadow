Staunton Vindicator: December 18, 1868

Go To Page : 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |

-Page 02-

[No Title]
(Column 04)
Summary: The paper praises a speech by John Garrett, President of the Baltimore and Ohio Railroad, that extols the financial benefit that could accrue from construction of the Valley Railroad. The editors urge Augusta County to subscribe $300,000 to the Valley Railroad and add to the $100,000 subscribed by Staunton and the $400,000 by Rockbridge County.

[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The Juvenile Musical Association gave a concert at the chapel of the Deaf, Dumb, and Blind Institution on Friday. It was a "splendid entertainment."
[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The proposed county subscription to the Valley Railroad was lost. The needed 3/5 majority failed by a small margin.
[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The U. S. Internal Revenue Collector will be in Staunton on December 22nd and 23rd to collect unpaid taxes.

Summary: The Virginia State bird hunting championship was held in Augusta on the 9th, 10th, and 11th of December. David N. Foster of Nelson defeated Capt. Garrett G. Gooch of Louisa.

Married
(Column 02)

Died

Died
