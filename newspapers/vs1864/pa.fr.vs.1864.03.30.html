Valley Spirit: 03 30, 1864

-Page 01-

Description of Page: Speech fills the entire page.
Speech of Hon. John L. Dawson of Pennsylvania on the State of the Union
(Column 1)
Summary: Prints concluding half of the speech given by John L. Dawson before the state legislature on February 24. In it, Dawson assails the Lincoln administration for executing the war in a way that will destroy the Union.

-Page 02-

Description of Page: Poetry and fiction, columns 1-4, reports of troop movement in Dalton, Georgia, column 5, classified ads, columns 5-6
The Spotted Fever
(Column 4)
Summary: Warns readers about the symptoms of spotted fever, which recently has taken the lives of thousands in Philadelphia.
The Johnstown Homicide
(Column 4)
Summary: Reports that Joseph Moore was convicted of murder in the second degree for the death of Mr. Marbourg. Moore was sentenced to six years in the penitentiary.
(Names in announcement: Mr. Marbourg, Joseph Moore)

-Page 03-

Description of Page: Report on the extent of Union loyalty in Louisiana, column 3
The Position of the Democracy
(Column 1)
Summary: Argues that the Democratic party is loyal to the Union, perhaps more so than the Republican party.
Unconditional Unionism
(Column 2)
Summary: Suggests that those men who support an "unconditional union," in which the federal government could wield substantially more power, are actually disunionists.
Call Upon the Leaguers
(Column 3)
Summary: Scoffs at attempts by an abolitionist newspaper to encourage members of abolition leagues and clubs to fight for the Union.
A Soldier
(Column 3)
Summary: Suggests that whiskey can explain why a soldier recently was spotted burning copies of a Democratic newspaper.
Democratic State Conventions
(Column 4)
Summary: Summarizes proceedings of the Democratic State Convention held on March 24 in Philadelphia. Reports that the Convention voted to support General George B. McClellan for President.
Fremont in the Field
(Column 5)
Summary: Suggests that the expected candidacy of General Fremont as a Republican candidate for President could derail the hopes of both Chase and Lincoln.
The State Quotas Under the Last Call for Volunteers
(Column 6)
Summary: Lists the most recent quotas established by Lincoln for the number of men needed to volunteer from each state. Pennsylvania's quota is 26,302.
Congressional
(Column 6)
Summary: Reports on the recent proceedings of the Confederate Congress, including the consideration of a bill to promote enlistments.

Street Lamps and Gas Light
(Column 1)
Summary: Asks that the person in charge of the gas lamps in the town's streets exercise common sense when deciding when to light the lamps. The editor says that the lights are often lit when the moon can provide plenty of light.

Clergyman on Trial
(Column 2)
Summary: Questions the punishment issued to Rev. Kepler by the Methodist Conference at Altoona for preaching the divinity of slavery. The editor suggests that, had he preached the divinity of miscegenation, he would have been embraced by the Conference.
(Names in announcement: Rev. Kepler)

(Column 2)
