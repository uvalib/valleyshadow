Pork
(Column 01)
Summary: The paper reports that "some little lots of pork have been offered in this market and sold at $10.00 per hundred."
[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: "Delighted audiences" attended the exhibition of the Miltonian Tableaux at the Town Hall on Friday and Saturday.
[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The paper reports that the jury acquitted John M. Stanley in the trial for the murder of Jacob Scherer.
(Names in announcement: John M. Stanley, Jacob Scherer)
[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The residence of Mrs. Irvine, near Deerfield, caught fire and burned on Saturday evening.
(Names in announcement: Mrs. Irvine)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: William J. Larew of Augusta and Miss Bettie P. Graham, daughter of Mrs. Susan C. Graham of Greenville, were married in Greenville at the residence of the bride's mother on November 2nd by the Rev. Samuel Lambeth.
(Names in announcement: William J. Larew, Bettie P. Graham, Susan C. Graham, Rev. Samuel Lambeth)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: J. W. T. Hawpe of Augusta and Miss Amanda Boone, daughter of Daniel Boone of Rockingham, were married near Port Republic at the residence of the bride's father on November 3rd by the Rev. Mr. Hawes.
(Names in announcement: J. W. T. Hawpe, Amanda Boone, Daniel Boone, Rev. Hawes)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: E. M. Brown, formerly of Staunton, and Miss Mattie Barry of Georgia were married in Georgia at the residence of Col. W. J. Anderson by the Rev. Mr. Russel.
(Names in announcement: E. M. Brown, Mattie Barry, Col. W. J. Anderson, Rev. Russel)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: Joseph M. Fawber and Miss Mollie J. Baylor, both of Augusta, were married on October 28th by the Rev. A. A. Bushong.
(Names in announcement: Joseph M. Fawber, Mollie J. Baylor, Rev. A. A. Bushong)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: J. Tuley Mitchell, Jr., of Augusta and Bettie W. Young of Nelson were married at the residence of the bride's brother on November 4th by the Rev. J. Witherson.
(Names in announcement: J. Tuley MitchellJr., Bettie W. Young, Rev. J. Witherson)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: Jefferson P. Coffman and Miss Catharine M. Cupp, both of Augusta, were married on November 2nd by the Rev. James M. Follansbee.
(Names in announcement: Jefferson P. Coffman, Catharine M. Cupp, Rev. James M. Follansbee)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: Prof. W. O. Trueheart of the Wesleyan Female Institute and Miss Sallie C. Myers of Jefferson County were married in Staunton at the Methodist Church by the Rev. W. A. Harris.
(Names in announcement: Prof. W. O. Trueheart, Sallie C. Myers, Rev. W. A. Harris)
