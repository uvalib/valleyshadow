[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: Disgusted with Senator Lewis because of his Radical views and collaboration with Republicans despite being elected by conservative men. Demands he resign, or the Legislature force him to resign.
Full Text of Article:

John F. Lewis our U. S. Senator, elected by and as the nominee of the conservatives of the Legislature, seems to take every opportunity to exhibit his ultra opposition to the Southern people. Allied actively with the ultras in Congress, meddling with the elections in Washington, threatening the Republicans in the Virginia Legislature, or opposing the nomination of Akerman of Georgia, because he served on Toombs' staff, while he for the same reason took a Confederate contract to make the iron, which dealt death and destruction to the ranks of his friends, he exhibits the same ultra Radicalism, and is not by any means the Representative of the sentiments of the State in the U. S. Senate. His pride should induce him to relinquish the position he holds and in which he must know that he misrepresents his people. Has not our Legislature sufficient back-bone to request him to resign?


Confederate Dead
(Column 03)
Summary: Talks about the formation of an Association for fixing up graveyards after the successful ceremonies honoring the war dead the week before. Records the proceedings of the meeting, officers appointed, committees formed and other motions passed.
(Names in announcement: Col. A. W. Harman, Capt. Charles D. McCoy, Col. James H. Skinner, Col. John B. Baldwin, Col. M. G. Harman, Col. A. W. Harman, Col. H. J. Williams, Col. John D. Lilley, Capt. W. L. Balthis, Capt. F. F. Sterrett, Capt. E. A. Fulcher, Lt. Silas H. Walker, Capt. F. M. Berkeley, Capt. Charles D. McCoy, Lt. Charles S. Arnall, Lt. Col. Bolivar Christian, Capt. James Bumgardner, Capt. John N. Opie, Capt. James H. Waters, S. Travers Phillips, R. Mauzy, W. L. Bumgardner, W. L. Balthis)
Full Text of Article:

On Saturday last, agreeably to announcement last week, a large number repaired to the Cemetery and did much in fixing up the graves of the Confederate dead buried there, while the Ladies furnished refreshments for those engaged in this labor of love.

By far the greatest good accomplished was the formation of an Association for fixing up and keeping in proper condition the Confederate Cemetery, as published below, and invite all sympathizers throughout the county to unite and act with this most commendable organization.

At a meeting of ex-Confederate soldiers and their friends, held, at the Cemetery, Staunton June 18, 1870, Col. A. W. Harman was called to the chair, and Capt. Charles D. McCoy, was requested to act as Secretary.

The chairman stated the object of the meeting to be the organization of a permanent society for the purpose of taking proper care of the graves of our comrades buried in this Cemetery.

On motion the chairman was directed to appoint a committee of five to report officers for the permanent organization. This committee having retired, returned and submitted the following report:

The committee appointed to report permanent officers of the Association, recommend the following:

For President, Col. Jas. H. Skinner, 52nd Infantry.

For Vice Presidents, Col. Jno. B. Baldwin, Col. M. G. Harman, Col. A. W. Harman, 12th Cavalry; Col H. J. Williams, 5th Infantry; Capt. W. L. Balthis, Staunton Artillery; Capt. F. F. Sterrett, 14th Cavalry; Capt. E. A. Fulcher, 10th Cavalry; Lieut. Silas H. Walker, 1st Cavalry; Capt. F. M. Berkeley, Adj't. Imboden's Brigade.

For Secretary, Capt. Chas D McCoy, 25th Infantry.

For Treasurer, Lieut. Chas S. Arnall, 5th Infantry.

For Central Executive Committee, with instructions to appoint associate Committees throughout the county, and to draft articles of association for the government of the society, Lt. Col Bolivar Christian, Chairman; Capt. Jas. Bumgardner, 52nd Infantry; Capt. John N Opie, 6th Cavalry; Capt. Jas H. Waters, 5th Infantry; S. Travers Phillips.

The Association will assemble at Staunton to receive the report of the committee on the articles of association, and to perfect the organization on such day as the President and Executive Committee shall appoint and publish.

R. MAUZY

B. CHRISTIAN

C. S ARNALL

W. L. BUMGARDNER

W. L. BALTHIS,(Committee)

After several motions to amend, which were all rejected, the report was adopted.

The meeting then adopted a resolution requesting the Staunton papers to publish these proceedings.

On motion the meeting adjourned.

A. W. CARMAN, Chairman.

CHARLES D. McCOY, Secretary.

Died
(Column 03)
Summary: Mrs. M. C. Koiner, wife of Cyrus Koiner, died near Fishersville on June 7th. She was 40 years old. She had been a long-time member of the Evangelical Lutheran Church, and, "during her protracted and painful illness, exemplified the virtues of true piety." She leaves a husband and children. The large number of people at her funeral served as a testament to her friendly character.
(Names in announcement: M. C. Koiner, Cyrus Koiner)
