Staunton Spectator: March 29, 1859

Go To Page : 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |

-Page 01-

Revival of Know-Nothingism
(Column 4)
Summary: Very sarcastic article about a conversation between black men, one of whom complains about the possibility of Africans being imported into Virginia. He complains that they are foreign and unnaturalized, and thus he refuses to associate with them. Conversation is written in dialect.

-Page 02-

Division of the State
(Column 1)
Summary: The Spectator is suspicious of John Letcher because he once proposed that the state be split so that slavery could be abolished in Western Virginia. While Letcher is "sound on the matter of slavery now," the paper worries that he might "still entertain a little of the old grudge against one portion of the state."
Ratification Meeting--County Candidates
(Column 02)
Summary: A meeting of Whigs and Americans of Augusta County was held at the Court House to ratify the Richmond nominations and bring out county candidates for the House of Delegates. Messers. Massie, McCue, and Christian were declared the candidates for the House.
