Franklin County: Peter Boyer to his father, Peter Boyer, March 30, 1865

Summary:
Boyer provides an account of Grant's armies in Virginia

Peter Boyer

March 30th 1865

Camp Remound

Dear Father

I take the pleasure to write a fiew lines to let you know that I am well at present hopeing that you are all the same I also let you know that we have news of a great victory down at Peters Burg but I don't think that John was in the fight as much as I can learn in the news paper the fifth corps was not engaged in the battle further I let you know that our cavalry is down with General Grants army that is all the mounted men but the dismounted men are still here I think we will stay here a while I also let you know that I sent

[page 2]
a letter home with twenty dollars I wish you would answer that letter as soon as possible and let me know wether you got that money and direct your letter to this place I will give you the direction at the end of this letter and our mail goes down to petersburg and if you don't direct them to this place then I wont get them if they go down there I also let you know that I sent my picture home a while ago inform me also about that and about sending home clothing I and Cyrus Engle and I will sent some home one of thes days further I let you know that we have bad weather at present it was raining all night

[page 3]
and is still raining to day I don't know any more news at present I hope to here from you soon Send stamps in your next letter I will close for this time

from your son

Peter Boyer

I will now give you the direction
Peter Boyer Co I 17th Pa Cav
Remound Camp
Plesent Valley MD
In Care of Major Spira

Cyrus Engle wants you to tell old Mrs Engle to send a dollar worth postage stamps and he will send her one dollar again That is all at present To my father
Peter Boyer
