Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, August 14, 1868

Summary:
Evans inquires whether or not Sibert has decided to go to the Springs with him. Because she did not mention it in her last letter, he fears she is declining, but urges her to decide in the affirmative.

Aug 14th 1868

Staunton

My Dear Mollie

You did not Say in your dear letter wether you would go to the Springs or not I hope you will go with me my love for I am compeld to go my self and I want you to go with me. I will be down a bout Seven O clock if I live. I think you had better make up your mind to go to the Springs you may have forgotten to menchen it in your letter. I thought as you did not menchen it that you declined

[page 2]
decline going. Dr Arthur will come with me Tomorrow. Good bye my love untill we meet tomorrow

Yours

David H Evans
