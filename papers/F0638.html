Franklin County: Josiah Bloss to His Sister, August 23, 1864

Summary:
Bloss writes that his brigade is fighting every day, and reports that he has had a letter from the hospital steward where their brother died, regarding their brother's wishes about his personal belongings.

His Sister

Aug. 23rd. 1864

Harpers Ferry

Dear Sister,

I received yours of the 17th. to day I was very Happy to Hear that yous was all well at Home, we had to fall back to Harpers Ferry as you can see through the papers, we are in General Sheridans Department, & General [unclear: Merrid] commands ar Division, Devins our Brigadier was wounded this week, and Col. Cisanola comands the Brigade at the present. we are having fighting every day.

I am detailed at Brigade Head Quarters [unclear: shoeing] for Head Quarters, I wont haft to go near a fight as long as I will be here, perhaps I will be here all sumer just acording to circumstances.

I had a letter from the Hospital Stuard where William died at the U.S.A. Hospital stating William wanted him to send his wach to me, but, as they was going to send his valice & sabre & revolvers home he thought it best to send the wach along with them, you tell Mother to Keep the wach untill I get Home, I also gave William a Revolver, a large Remmington Revolver, his own Revolver is silver mounted, you will also keep the one I gave him till I get Home, and his sabre and Revolver you will hang up in his room in memory of our Dear beloved Brother, as his sword has done his duty.



[page 2]
I send forty Dollars to you as I wrote to before, you will receive it from Fuller in Scranton. I am well an in good spirits.

from your Brother

Josiah Bloss

Address as before
P.S. I made a present of a Revolver to James Ferry and one to Joshua Ferry, tell James & Joshua Ferry that I am very much obliged to them for there trouble in conveying Williams body Home untill I can Pay them a better tribbute.
J. Bloss
