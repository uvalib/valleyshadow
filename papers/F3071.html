Franklin County: David Demus to Mary Jane Demus, October 15, [1863?]

Summary:
Demus expresses disappointment that Mary Jane has not written recently and urges her to write again.

Mary Jane Demus

riton the 15th of october

Mor ilon SCarlia

Mi Dear

i mus set down this morn and tell you What i think a boute you fer i now verry Well that if you thot as much of me as i dou of you Wiy you Wod righte more then Whot you dou fer i have rote fore letter and hant got one yet and i Wod like to now What is the [unclear: reachen] that you doan righte more then What you do and let me now how you ar fer [unclear: thar] is all the reste of the boys geting letter evr time that the male Cum in but if you ant Able to righte Wiy i shal not think harde a boute i but if you ar Well and doant righte Wiy i shal give oup too if you had much fealing fer me Wiy you Wod right to me fer i Cant get paper oneley som times but i hope that you Will pleas to righte Wons more and let me now how you ar and all the reste pleas to give mi love to all the frends i hope that you Will [unclear: Cape] this letter for to look at fer i am geting a long verrey Well at this time i am Waying on offerser and i have [unclear: now] harde [unclear: think] to Dow

Directe yor letter to morris ilon SC
54 mastues
Col redment
Comey
to

yor Dear frend

David Dimes
