Freedmen's Bureau Records: W. L. Mowry to Thomas P. Jackson, August 5, 1867

Summary:
Mowry, the Deputy Sheriff of Fishersville, informs Jackson that several people have been arrested, and that his presence is requested at the trial.

[written by Thomas P. Jackson] Notifies of arrest of certain parties (viz. A. Greaver, P Bowls (c) & Joshua Hill (c) & trial I am wanted at examination Maj. T. P. Jackson

August 5th /67

Fishersville

Dr. Sir

Upon [deleted: the] a warrant issued by A. E. Pierce a Justice of the Peace I have arrested the parties - they demand your presence therefore we will defer the trial until you come.

W. L. Mowry, D.S.
