Augusta: J. S. Calvert to Alexander H. H. Stuart, January 21, 1868

Summary:
 J. S. Calvert describes financial matters for Alexander H. H. Stuart.

Treasury Office of Virginia, Richmond Hon. A. H. H. Stuart

January 21st, 1868

Richmond

Dr sir

Yours of the 18th inst is before me, and I submit the following as the result of the examination you desired--
1866. January 1. Your credit in stocks 29.700
'' Feby 29 Transfered to W.L.C. Trustee 26.000
leaving in your name $3.700
The Int. due 1 January '66 on 29.700 -- $1782.
Funded A. H. H. Stuart on 3.700 222
'' W L Clark 720
Subject to order of W. B. Clark 480 1422
leaving to credit of somebody $360
This, I presume will be claimed by W. L. Clark on the $3000 transfd to him Jany 18. 1867.

The Int. for the year 1866 [added: on 26,000] is not funded -- and standing to credit of W. L. G. Trustee -- $1560. The certificates for the 2% deferred interest have issued to W. L. C.

Very Truly Yrs

J. S. Calvert
