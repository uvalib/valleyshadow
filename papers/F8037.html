Franklin County: A. N. Rankin to Simon Cameron, September 16, 1856

Summary:
Rankin writes Cameron about local party politics.

Franklin Repository and Transcript
Terms $2. Per Annum In Advance
Five Copies $9. Ten Copies $15.
Circulation 3,500. A. N. Rankin
Sep. 16,/56

Sep. 16 1856

Chambersburg Pa.

Hon S. Cameron
Dear Sir

I have been so much engaged since receiving your's that I could not till now find time to answer. I very well remember meeting with you in Harrisburg, but supposed you had entirely forgotten me.

I have been twice around

[page 2]
the County, once to secure the nomination of one of my partners, for this office of State Senator. And once to secure the right person to fill a vacancy that occurred in our Assembly Ticket.

In both instances I was successful.

Our county the Birthplace of Buchanan is truly the battle-ground

[page 3]
we will have to fight for every inch of ground we get.

I Love the American Party and especially the Democratic portion of it. I joined the order from the Democracy. We have gained between 2 and 5 hundred of that kind of votes and lost about 200 Catholic & Foreign votes.

I will be in Harrisburg the last of this or the first of next week.

Truly Your Friend

A. N. Rankin
