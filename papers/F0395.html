Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Louisa Geisel, August 15, 1863

Summary:
Geisel writes his family about his unit being removed from action so that it can rebuild at a site south of Washington.

Louisa Geisel

August 15th

Washington D.C.

Dear Sister

I will write you a few lines to let you know that I am well and alive yet, and I hope that this may find you all in good health. Our Brigade was relieved from active service, we left the front at the Rappahannock on thursday and came by railroad to Washington where we arrived on friday morning about 10 clock. We layed at the soldiers rest in Washington till 12 O'clock yesterday when we were marched about 5 miles below Washington on the Maryland side. The weather was so hot that the most of our men gave out along the road and they have not all came up yet. We will encamp somewhere around Washington to get recruited

[page 2]
up again. Our whole Brigade only numbered 763 men for duty when we were relieved our Regt. [illeg.] We lost 5 men of our Co. in the fight on the 1rst [illeg.] near Culpepper 2 killed and 3 wounded. I seen our wounded yesterday. they are doing well. I seen John Markley about the 23 of July at Manassas gap. he looked well, but I had no opportunity to talk to him as both our Regt. were marching passed each other. I can write no more at present, but you will soon hear from me again. Remember me to all inquiring friends, and write soon.

I remain your affectionate Brother

Christian Geisel

Co.H 6th Pa. Cavalry Washington D.C.
