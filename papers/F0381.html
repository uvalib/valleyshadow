Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Louisa Geisel, February 12, 1862

Summary:
Geisel thanks his sister for the food and letters that the family has sent.

Louisa Geisel

Feb. 12th 1862

Dear Sister!

I write to you these few lines, hoping that they may find you in as good health as they leave me in at present. I received the letter which Father [unclear: send ] to me, and the box arrived here a few days afterwards. your Spunchcakes, which you [unclear: send ] were excellent, and the sausage were something new to us. they

[page 1]
tasted first rate; and i am very much obliged to you for your trouble and kindness. I suppose you were looking for a letter from me for some time, but i hope you will excuse me for not writing sooner for i did not discover the letter which you [unclear: send ] to me in the box until last sunday; i had several letters and some papers in the box, and by looking over them last sunday i found, to my astonishment, a letter sealed up with my name on it, which i had not seen before, and after opening it i found it was from you. i would have wrote to you right away, but as you said you would like to see my likeness, i thought i would get it first, which i did today. i wrote a letter to Polly Furhman last week. when you write to me again tell me wether she said anything about it,

[page 2]
and how they are getting along in your neighbourhood. i think you had better put Polly on the old maids list if she dondt get married before long. We are in our old camp yet near Washington. Levi Markley and, some relations of Markley's, whose name i do not know, were here to see me yesterday. Levi is well and all the rest from our neighbourhood, except Haberstroh, he was taken sick some time ago, and they think he [unclear: wont ] get over it. We have had fine weather for a few days and the ground is drying of mud and if it keeps on this way a few weekks, i think we will make a move further south, but we do not know yet how soon we are going to move. I heard today that they had a battle in North Carolina and

[page 3]
our men gained the victory. in our neighbourhood everything is quiet. this is all i can write at present. i am well and hope that this letter may find you all the same. give my best respects to all who may inquire after me. remember me to all the young folks in the neighbourhood and write to me soon.

your affectionate brother

Christian Geisel

the directions: Christian Geisel, Co. H, 6th regiment, Pa. Cavalry, Col. R. Rush D.C.
