Franklin County: Peter Boyer to Daniel Boyer, June 25, 1863

Summary:
Boyer gives a description of an engagement between Federal and Confederate troops in Maryland.

Daniel Boyer

June 25th 1863

Dear brother

I take the present opportunity to in form you that I received your [unclear: letter] and I was very glad to here of you further I let you know that we came to the regiment yesterday morning I let you know that when we broke our camp up at brooks station all the dismounted men were sent to [unclear: safeguard] court house there we got horses again and from there we marched by Alexantria There we stayed a short time then we got again marching order when we started of and crossed the Petomac river into maraland then we went up on the other side the river about forty miles to gard a bridge we marched one day and one night before we stopped and in the morning we came to the bridge there was a party of rebel there they were found in line of battle we were also formed to charge but before

[page 2]
we came in sight of the rebs they retreated across the river we then stayed there two days and two nights then we started again for alexantria when we came to that place we stayed there two days then we started for our regiment I let you know that I couldent get any leters while we were from the regiment and had [unclear: hardly] to send one of the regiment lies three miles from alexantria I also tell that I was sick while we were at [unclear: Stofards] I had headache and direar I got meadacine from the doctor and I got well again I also tell you that our regiment was in two fights during our absens but there is only one man wounded in our company

I also did see in your leter about I don't think that I go to the girls

[page 3]
until I came home I also let you know that my money is all gone the time when I was sick I spented [unclear: a good many by the] [illeg.] and I wish you would send a few dolars I do not know when we will get paid but I ges we will get paid before long I also let you know that I received the postage stamps wich you sent to me

So I must come to a close for this time

write soon

Peter Boyer

to Daniyel Boyer
