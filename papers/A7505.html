Augusta County: William L. Evans to David H. Evans, March 16, 1864

Summary:
Evans writes to his brother to ask for help in conducting some personal business.

March 16 1864

Camp Stonewall - Prepano

D Bro

I Recd a letter from Home toDay and on the outside it looks like you [unclear: address] it and if you did I cant see [unclear: why] you did not write to me

I think the letter was sent to Staunton and you put the [unclear: Envlop] on it I sent you by (Sergt Lyan) a letter and I wish you would [unclear: answer] it if you cant why I will get som one else to do it

I Hope you will answe this and [unclear: send] me what Information [illeg.] asked you to [illeg.] and let me kno if you can't [illeg.] that [deleted: ] if those [unclear: shoes] comes to [unclear: Staunton] [unclear: only] you keep them until I send for them [illeg.] Dont let anyone steel them Write Immediately My love to all

Yr Bro

W L Evans
