Franklin County: Diary of James E. Green (1863)

1863

June 1863

21st

Cloudy this morning

My health is not good. I had Diearah for several days past. Eavning there was Batizeing in Antietem the Eavning. 12 or 13 men belonging to Ramseurs Brigade was Baptized. it is the first Soldiers I have seen Baptized since Ive been in service, & its a nice thing to see Soldiers baptized. We still remain here 1 mile off from Hegerstown. a prosspect of more Rain this Eavning- no more to day.

22nd June.

We left Camp at 5 A.M. & moved off through Hegerstown & took the Direct Road for Harisburg the Capital of Pencilvania. and marched till 1/2 past 1 in the Eavning & stoped to Camp at or near Greencastle Pa. we Crossed the Pa. line at 11 Oclock A.M. at a little village called Middleburg. The People over here looks on us with a heavy brou. they appeare very grum indeed. we are encamped to Night neare Greencastle some 5 or 6 miles into Pa. some Rain to night. my health not good yet. the same disease above mentioned.

23d

I here no chat of leaving here yet. the Weather cool this morning it seames to be some for 10 miles to the Aleganez Range of Mountains on our left from this place. J.W. Collins & J.W. Bennett did leave Camp without Compatent authority the Eavning of the 22nd. soon after arriveing at this place. suposed to be Deserted but not known yet. We Remained here till 1/2 after 4 morning of the

24th

We left this Camp & Marched off through Greencastle & on to Chambersburg the yanks left day before yesterday. we expected to have a fight with them here but they left before we got there. We marched through Town the People looked on us very grum. they had nothing to say to us only when we spoke to them. but they appeared very liberel in feeding us, I think it was not out of good will but purely out of feare. for they think we will treat as our Enemys do us in Eastern N. C. & other places, burn them out &c &c.

We come on some 3 miles out of Town & Camped. Gen Daniels Brigade was scatered out on Picket to night. We thought the Enemy would make a stand here but they did not, So we dont know where they intend to fight us. neither do I know - who fare we will go if we dont get a fight for we are now some 20 Miles in Pa. & no fight of mutch importance yet, only at Winchester & our Division was not in it. It was Johnsons Division that Routed them from there. Run them out of there Breastworks & captured 6000 men with there arms & 64 peaces of Artilery, & all there Commisary Stores &c. Johnsons Division belongs to the Emels Corps, the same to which this (ie) Roads Division belongs Our men gets there Rations of a Eav. sometimes in the night, so they are up or some of them all night, after marching all day.

25th June

we were aroused by the long Roll at 12 Oclock this morning took the Road (ie) the Turnpike for Shippensburg. as one Brigade (viz) Daniels to take possession of the Town. for our Cavalry run the yank out the Eav before & wanted to to go on. so we marched in quick time to said place, the distance of 10 or 12 miles. We arrived her this morning at 7 the People of Shippensburg looked mad at us as usual in this Country. this is a fine Country the fields all covered with the finest Wheat I ever saw. Clover, Herds grap, Oats, Corn &c. &c. this is a thickly settled Country, and a bondance of little Towns- The buildings is of Brick & Stone chiefly. The growth is Oak, Hickory & Black Walnut. And the People Generly Ugly. they are a mixed People, Dutch, Irish, &c. I dont know how long we will stay here, nor where we will go when we leave here. We will be apt to wait here untill the Rest of our Division comes up with us. there has been a bondance of rain lately it Rains every day or two.

26th

We lay all day here at Shippensburg. The rest of our Division came up to day. they were all wet for it has been raining all day. We Remained here till 6 A.M.
