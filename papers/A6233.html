Augusta County: William L. Evans to David H. Evans, October 3, 1868

Summary:

H. Evans & Bro.
Wholesale Dealers in
Tobacco, Cigars, Snuffs, Papers, &c.

October 3 1868

Dear Bro,

After I Wrote the letter on Yesterday I have Come to the Conclusion that We had better not start business in Staunton as John is opposed to us and Will do all he can against us= if he was favorable to us We Could do better= I think it is selfish in him I will not think as Much of him hear after= let us try some other Point= I would give the room up if I was You= but if you want to go in I will put half the Mony in and I will Keep My situation and if it pays I will then come to Staunton= Write Me What you think ofit= them Factrys will be a big Drawback to Our Business up there

Truly

Wm L Evans



[page 2]

We can Wait untill spring= and then perhaps We Will do better
Truly
Wm L Evans
