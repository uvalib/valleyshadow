Franklin County: Joseph Hills Clintock to Adjutant General Thomas, March 4, 1863

Summary:
Dr. Joseph Hills Clintock certifies that John Ault remains unfit for duty for at least another thirty days.

Adjutant Gen. Thomas USA

March 4, 1863

Chaplain John Ault of the 126th Regt Penn. Vols. having applied and received leave of absence from Maj. Genl. Joseph Hooker, I do hereby certify that I have examined this officer, and find that he is recovering, but is, as yet closely confined to his bed and absolutely unable to travel to rejoin his regiment. He has been absent since the 17th of January 1863, and will not be able to return in less than thirty days from this date. dated at London, Franklin Co., Penna. this 4th day of March 1863.

Jos. Hills McClintock
Attending Physician

A. B. See former certificate for description of his disease.

I hereby certify that Joseph H. McClintock, the signer of the above certificate, is, what he represents himself to be, a regular Practising Physician, and the attending Physician of Chaplain John Ault.

Jos. Crawford
