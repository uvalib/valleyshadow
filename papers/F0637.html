Franklin County: Josiah Bloss to His Sister, August 6, 1864

Summary:
Bloss writes his sister about their brother William's illness and how it is impossible for him to visit or bring his brother home.

His Sister

Aug. 6th. 1864

Washington City, D.C.

Dear Sister,

I received yours of the 4th to day. I am sory to tell you that I cant go to see William. we arived here last night in Washington. and we are going to day to Maryland to drive the invading rebels from the boaders of Pennsylvania. I expect we will have a fight with them tomorrow. I am about two hundred and sixty miles from William now. and I cant go to see him on any account that I can fix it. we where three days on the water coming here with our horses on board. I cant do anything for William whatever, so you will have to do the best that you can to have him home, take what money I have home and do the best with it you can in having him home & if it dont reach

[page 2]
borow money and I will Pay all expencess. I have six month Pay coming to me now and I expect to get Pay in a few days or two weeks.

I am well and in good health hoping that this will find you the same.

from your Brother

J. Bloss

my address - Co. K. 17th. Pa. Cavalry
2nd. Brig. 1st. Cav. Div.
Washington, D.C.
