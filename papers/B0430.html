Freedmen's Bureau Records: Thomas P. Jackson to J. T. H. Hall, January 16, 1868

Summary:
Jackson again writes to Hall about James Woods' appeal and his inability to secure the necessary papers from his lawyer.

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands,
Office 4th Division, 9th Sub-District, Virginia. Capt. J.T.H. Hall
Mil. Comr.
Woodstock

Jan 16th 1868

Staunton, Va.

Captain

I have the honor to transmit enclosed herewith copies of letters referring to appeal of James Woods, Elizabeth Furnace, Augusta Co. Va. Woods has again called at the office and prays the papers be referred to proper officer in Rockbridge Co. who can obtain the asked for papers from Mr. James Dorman.

Very Respectfully
Yr obt servt

Thos P. Jackson
Agent
