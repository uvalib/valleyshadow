Franklin County: Aaron Freher to Major, April 26, 1865

Summary:

Major

April 26th, 1865

I shall give you all the information I can in regard to Corpl. Altymier. He was missing at the Battle of "Hatcher's Run" on the 6th day of February 1865. I have never been able to say whther he was taken Prisoner or Killed. He was with the company on going into the fight and [unclear: when] we was driven back late in the evening. It is very probable that he was taken prisoner. I had 3 (three) missing in my co. Never heard from any of them. However, I don't think he was killed. We advanced over the same ground next day and would very probable have seen him. I am sorry for him. He was a noble soldier, miss him very much in the company. We have had a very exciting time a few days back there was great excitement when Lee surrendered his Army of Northern Virginia. I think the war is about at an end now. We have just recd. an official Dispatch of the Surrender of Johnson's Army. Great cheering going on.
