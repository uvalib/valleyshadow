Freedmen's Bureau Records: John Crawford to Thomas P. Jackson, July 22, 1867

Summary:
Crawford writes Jackson in reference to a lot in Waynesboro and the claim of a Mr. Morrison to it.

Freedmans Bureau
Mr. Jackson at Staunton

July 22nd /67

Waynesboro

Mr. Jackson

I talked to Mr. Morrison [added: and wife] this morning [added: he said] that if they had an doubt he could show anyone that he could give a good right to the lot, and if they are not certefied they can take the building off when they get ready.

Yours &c

John L. A. Crawford
Trustee

P.S. Baldwin is not to blame, he was overpowered by the balance of the Committee. It is out of his power at this time for him to come to see you but as soon as he can he will come.
