Freedmen's Bureau Records: Thomas P. Jackson to Cyrus Alexander, May 30, 1867

Summary:
Jackson asks Alexander if he knows of anyone who can provide transportation for an elderly man and his disabled daughter.

Bureau R.F & A L
Off 4 Div. 9 S. Dist Va Dr. Alexander

May 30/67

Waynesboro

Sir

I have in store rations for Eliza ---- and her family recommended by you and other colored persons amtg to 5 full rations also for an old man William Brown & his daughter (crippled) White persons

Cannot some charitably disposed person transport same from this place to Waynesboro. Please arrange if you can.

Yr obt servt

Thos P Jackson
Agent
