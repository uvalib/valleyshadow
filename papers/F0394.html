Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Louisa Geisel, June 20, 1863

Summary:
Geisel writes to his family about the multiple moves his unit has made in recent weeks to respond to the shifting position of Confederate forces.

Louisa Geisel

June 20th 1863

Fairfax Courthouse

Dear Sister

I take the present opportunity to write you a few lines hoping that they may find you all in good health. I wrote you a few lines on the 13th at Catletts Station, but I had no opportunity to send it as we marched from there that evening. I suppose you have heard of the cavalry fight we had at Beverly Ford on the 9th[illeg.] Our Regt. was engaged there and we lost 109 men killed wounded and missing. Since that time we have been moving nearly all the time. We went from Catletts Station to Thoroughfare Gap, from there to Manasses Junction, then to Bull Run

[page 2]
from Bull run we marched up towards Leesburg and encountered the rebel army at Adlia at which place we left all our cavalry yesterday, it is about 20 miles from here "Fairfax C. House." Regt. was sent with our supply train, we expect to get [illeg.] tonight. most of Hookers army is above fairfax, some of them were sent up to our cavalry last night. we expect to have heavy fight shortly again. By all accounts some of the rebel cavalry is in Penn. again. and I expect that there is great excitement up there now, but I dondt think they will stay there long. I can write no more at present. Give my respects to all inquiring friends.

Your affectionate Brother.

Christian Geisel

Please excuse haste and mistakes.
