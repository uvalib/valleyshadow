Franklin County: Christian Geisel to William Geisel, February 2, 1862

Summary:
Geisel writes to his brother about guard duty during the winter along the Rappahannock.

William Geisel

February 2nd 1862

Banks of Rappahannock River, Snowdon Plantation

Dear Brother William

I received your welcome letter of Jan. 9th enclosed in a letter from sister Louisa, and as I answered her letter soon after I received it, I suppose you have been informed that I received yours also. I have also wrote a few lines to Father since, which I hope he has received.

We are still on guard along the Rappahannock River. last week we had a snowstorm here, the snow was about a foot deep, but it is all melted by this time, and the weather is very pleasant. I suppose you must have sleighing up there. I have not much news to tell you, our army is all laying in their camps, and the roads is so bad that I dondt think, they

[page 2]
will move for a while; for my part I am very well satisfied here, as long as our army lays here, but I wish the war was over. I heard yesterday that a good many men of this part of our army will get furloughs for ten days, but I dondt think we will get any, while we are on provost guard, but if there is any chance of getting one, I will apply for one, for I would be very glad to you all. I must now bring my letter to a close, hoping that it may find you all in as good health as I enjoy at present. Give my kindest regards to Father, mother, brothers, and sisters and my compliments to all inquiring friends. I hope to have the pleasure soon to hear from you again.

[page 3]

I remain your affectionate Brother

Christian Geisel

Co. H 6th Penn. cavalry Rush's Lancers Head. Q. Gen. Smith. Vir
