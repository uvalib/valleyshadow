Augusta County: John Scott to E. P. Smith, January 1, 1869

Summary:
 James W. Cochran, a local black from Mt. Solon, has proposed to start a school in his town, and Scott writes to say that he will travel to the locale as soon as weather permits.

Rev E P Smith Staunton Va

Jan 1/69

Dear Sir

Yours of the 30th came duly in which you say that you will send a man for Mt Solon as soon as transportation comes

I have not heard from that point since the application made by Mr Jas W. Cochran (colored) the other day for a teacher

I will go down to Mt. Solon tomorrow if I can, and find out how school matters are--would go to day were it not for rain which has made it uncertain about my being to able to ford the river.

Will report immediately thereafter.--Jan 2 Dont believe I can get to Mt. Solon to day, but I want to see the place myself if I can before you send on

[page 2]
a teacher. Have written there. The name of the colored man who made application for a teacher is James W. Cochran. Mt. Solan.

I dont think it possible that any arrangement has been made for that place but still I havnt heard

Very Respectfully

John Scott
