Augusta County: John B. Baldwin to John McCue, May 27, 1861

Summary:
Baldwin states that he has "no position" to which he can appoint McCue in the military.

Inspector General's Office

May 27 1861

Head Quarters, Richmond,

My Dear Sir.

I regret that I have in my gift or under my control no position in which I could employ your capacities as a military man. I am satisfied you have the natural gifts of a soldier & that you will in any field win for yourself reputation & promotion if you get a fair chance.

As far as my opinion can avail as the result of a life long acquaintance I am bound to testify in your behalf.

Very truly
Your friend

John B. Baldwin

John H. McCue Esq.
