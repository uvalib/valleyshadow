Franklin County: Peter Boyer to his father, Peter Boyer, May 13, 1865

Summary:
Boyer is upset that his regiment is not being discharged.

Peter Boyer

May 13th, 1865

Camp Remound Chapel Point

Dear Father

I take the present pleasure to let you know that I am well at present hoping you all the rest are the same Further I let you know that we are still at this place There are rumers every day that we would leave I am very much disapointed about staying out hear so long if there was any fighting fights I wouldn't think of going home but now battles are fought and they [illeg.] but Victory's are won and now they keep us still out hear joust for nothing and make us drill evry day We are now out here nearly three years and since we are out hear there was nothing but fighting almost all [deleted: ] the time

[page 2]
and we have always done our duty and evry fight we was in we done our share and never was at home once but now the battles are fought and the rebels are driven away now they dont care for the poor soldier when he gets home or if he gets home at all last summer when the rebels were at the gates of washington there [unclear: was] fighting were glad for us then they had a great cheer about Sheridan's cavalry then they were almost scared to death when they hard a few shots fired but now they are out of danger Now they hate us when they see us I don't know when they will do us that favor to muster us out and pay us of but I heard last night that we would be discharged untill October I don't think we

[page 3]
will get home before our time is out The paper says all those that there time is out till the first of October will be discharged now, but the rest has to stay till there time is out and they our regiment will be discharged from the time when it was organised and it was organised some time in October I also let you know that the soldiers are getting very rugh they charged on a [deleted: ] last night and took every thing he had I dont know any more news at the present time write soon and direct Company 17th Pa Cav Chapel Point MD in care of Major [unclear: Spira Eckahart] get no letters since I am here the go all into the Regiment and I dont know where we will to the regiment So nothing more for this time

from your son

Peter Boyer

Excuse my bad writing my pen is so bad
write soon and dont forget it To my father
Peter Boyer
