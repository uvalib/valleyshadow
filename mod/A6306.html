Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, June 16, 1868

Summary:
Evans writes that he enjoyed his last visit and urges Sibert to visit Ella in Staunton next week. He also reports on family news.

June 16 1868

Staunton

My Dear Mollie

I arrived home Safely & had quite a pleasant ride. Though I did Not Enjoy it as much as the ride we had up the River in the Evening. I did Enjoy it So much. I did wish you could have come back with me. Ella will move this week & why can't you come up with me when I came down again you & Ella can then go up to the Furnace together it would be such a pleasant trip for you you could Spend a week or so in the mountains

[page 2]
very pleasantly I would Not want you to Stay too long for I would rather for you to Spend all your time in Staunton. I think you told me Some time ago that you intended to Spend the Summer with Ella I hope you will & make up your mind to come back with me Saturday a week if we live won't you? I know your Ma will Not object to your coming

You ask me Several times if I enjoyed my visit Yes and I always Enjoy my visits I am always happy when with you my love the greatest pleasure I have in this world is visiting you

[page 3]
at your home never think I am not happy or don't Enjoy myself for I do, & I love you with all the strength of my Soul & mind I love you with true devotion of my heart. Magnus & Ella will be down Saturday if they live. Tell your Ma Mr Sheets said he would take Miss Maggie's Photographs down with him today my love to your Ma. love to all

I Send a loving Kiss to you. I love you with all my heart & Soul may God Bless you. Good bye

Yours

D H Evans
