Franklin County: David A. Demus to Mary Jane Demus, November 28, 1864

Summary:
Demus comments on the value of his wife's letters and informs her that he has arrived in Hilton Head, South Carolina.

Mary Jane Demus

November the 28th 1864

hilton head south Carolina

My Dear Wife

i take my pen in hand to inform you that i am Well at present and i hope When these few lines reach you they may find you Well and i hope that you [unclear: inJoys] yourself Well on your visit and i [unclear: lad] three Days in [unclear: gork] before i got off and all the [unclear: firdes] i have got is to hilton head and i Can't tell When i Will get to the regiment and there is no boats going away for some time but i Just got here today and i thought i Would write and let you know that i got out safe and i feel very Well only it is very hot here but i hope that you Will excuse this this bad writing for i have nothing to write on but i Just hold the paper in my hand and move it as i write but i Wanted to write so bad that i just wrote as i Could i thought i Would write but i shall write a fool letter in a few Days When i get home and Can set down and write i so hope that you Will give my love to all the inquiring friends and my best love to yourself a my Dear when i feel so bad Why i Just look at Dear little face a how happy i feel but a how sorry i Was When i had to part With you but if i had a known that it Would go so hard With me for to leave you Why i Would not a Come home till i Could a Come to stay but the time Will soon run around but if i live to get home again Why all the people in the state Won't get me a to go again but i hope that you Will write some lovely letters to me so i Can set down a read them With the greatest of pleasure a my Dear how sorry i feel that i Can't set down and write a full letter nothing more at present but still remain

your Dear husband

David A Demus

Direct your letter to morris island Mary Jane Demus
