Augusta: Franklin Carroll to Virginia Miller, August 28, 1864

Summary:
Carroll writes to request clothes and suspects that his prior mailing has been interfered with.

The 28 1864
August

Camp Chase Ohio

Mrs. Virginia Miller

having received a letter from you the 28 day of July and answered immediately you stated in your letter you would send me some articles necessary for my comfort my wants are Needy at The Time I want you if you can send a pair pants shoes Hat Shirts Drawers and a coat if you can send me [added: the] articles I stand in so much need of you shall Be duly rewarded for them as soon as time will admit a grey color is the Kind allowed to come in on something singular to that

[page 2]
Send them as soon as you can send them By Express Franklin Carroll Prison No B Camp Chase Ohio in care of Colonel Richardson commanding I also understand that there was another man in this prison wrote to you for clothing also I thought in all probability he had interfered But as to that I cannot say PS I have a friend here S W Stuart who Needs a pair shoes if you can send 2 pairs you shall Be Paid as Early as possible

Your True Friend

Franklin Carroll
