Augusta County: A. D. Long to Cynthia Long, March 9, 1864

Summary:
Long writes to his sister about expected troop movement and his regrets of not having visited her on his time off.

March 9th 1864

Camp Randolph

Dear Sister

I take my pen in hand to inform you I am well i hope when these few lines approaches you they may find you enjoying the Same blessing they are all well at home I was at home 8 days not long Ago but i had not time to come down to See you I would have liked very much to have visited you all i promised fancy to come down: when i get A furlough i expect to come. i want you to write to me whenever you can. i have not much to write at this present time. we just came off picket day before yesterday. i didn't think it will be long until we will have to march. The Summer campaign will soon open. The Yankees can't lay Still until Spring. the weather is very good. i Saw uncle Sam A few days Ago he is well and looks very well & i hope you are getting along very well. i Expect to write A letter to fancy before long i must write one to Frank this evening. So i must close for

[page 2]
regards to all my friends. So nothing more.

remember your Brother up till dith

A D. Long

REN
B.F. Long Diea
Rev. 12th 1864
Near Cedar Creek
Shenandoah County
Virginia
