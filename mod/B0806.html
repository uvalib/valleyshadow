Freedmen's Bureau Records: Thomas P. Jackson to John A. McDonnell, June 3, 1867

Summary:
Jackson forwards criminal and school reports to McDonnell, referencing a case against a man named Hill that was settled unfavorably.

Bureau Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Office 4th Division 9th Sub-District Virginia Captain John A. McDonnell
Sub Assistant Commissioner

June 3rd 1867

Winchester

Captain

I have the honor to transmit Report to Bureau Affairs Criminal Affairs (Freedmen) and School affairs for this Division of Your District In relation to the case of Commonwealth v. Hill I respectfully remark that Defendant's friends are not satisfied with the conviction on the evidence addressed but his counsel declines taking any steps to set aside the judgement and await action from Hill through his personal friend before making any detailed or protest to you

Your obedient servant

Thos P Jackson
Agent
