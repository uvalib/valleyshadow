Augusta County: Robert Cowan to John H. McCue, November 18, 1857

Summary:
Robert Cowan requests that he be repaid, because he "never was harder run for money" than at that time.

November 18/57

Staunton

Dear Sir,

Enclosed you have statement of your account showing amount due me this date of $68 54/100.

I never was harder run for money than I am at this time.-- A note of mine from Philadelphia will fall due in bank in a few days & I lack considerably of the amount to pay it with.

Now if you can let me have the above amount, you will confer a great favor on

Yours Respectfully

Robert Cowan
