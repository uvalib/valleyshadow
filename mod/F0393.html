Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Franklin Geisel, June 6, 1863

Summary:
 Christian Geisel writes to his younger brother Franklin to inquire about his life and ask him about a new horse.

Franklin Geisel

June 6th 1863

Camp near Catletts Station

Dear Brother Franklin

I was very [illeg.] of may 30th and to hear that you were all well. As I received your letter with Sister Louisa's. I thought I would also send you a few lines again with her letter, which i hope may find you all in as good health as I enjoy at present. I expect you have grown a good deal since I saw you last, as I see by your letter you bear on the plough now which William used to do for me. Have you got the old horses yet, Thom, Perry and Bill? I have a little gray secesh pony now, I wish I could give you a ride on him, he is quiet as a little dog, I call him Taylor

[page 2]
Give my love to Willimina and Frad and William and the rest. I can write no more this time and will be happy soon to hear from you again. From

Your affectionate Brother

Christian Geisel

Co. H 6th Penn. Cavalry, Army of the Potomac Franklin P. Geisel [illeg.] Bucks County Penn.
