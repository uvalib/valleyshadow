Franklin County: T. P. Bucher to Edward McPherson, December 5, 1859

Summary:
Reverend T. P. Bucher sends his best wishes to Edward McPherson during his term as a Congressman and encourages him to remember God while serving his nation.

Honorable Edward McPherson, Member of Congress
Washington, D.C.

December 5. 59.

Gettysburg,

Dear Sir:

As I was not in town on the morning of your departure for Washington City, (and I feel a just pride, as a Citizen of your hometown, that it is honored with a Representative in the Halls of National Legislation.) by this means, I express my regards for your personal welfare, adding the pleasure I would realize in hearing of your distinguished mention among your [unclear: compears] in the Congress of the U. S. Our acquaintance has not yet assumed the form of personal friendship, nevertheless, as a Minister of the Gospel, my heart says unto me, "wish him well, let your prayers, and benedictions be for him & upon him [added: whilst] in that Metropolis of Government, and of sudden violence, and immorality, the Capitol of our Country." Don't forget your God, in your devotion to your Country!

With great Respect, I am Yours [illeg.]

T. P. Bucher
