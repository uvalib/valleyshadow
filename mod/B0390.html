Freedmen's Bureau Records: Thomas P. Jackson to John A. McDonnell, November 6, 1867

Summary:
Jackson writes about Benjamin Frazier, an impoverished man with family in Fredericksburg. He details the Bureau's efforts to locate Frazer's family and to get them to send him some help.

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands,
Office 4th Division, 9th Sub-District, Virginia Captain John A. McDonnell
Sub Assistant Commissioner
Winchester

November 6th 1867.

Staunton, Virginia

Captain

Under date July 3/67 I forwarded to your Head Quarters request of Benjamin Frazier (colored) for help from his family living near Fredericksburg, which was returned to me through your Head Quarters August 14/67 attention invited to endorsement of Lieutenant Hector Sears Assistant Sub Assistant Commissioner 1st Division 6 Sub-District Virginia August 8/67 who said that "Nathan his son would send him some money soon." Benjamin calls again at this office, is in a distressed condition and asks that Nathan send what he can of money as soon as possible or have some arrangements made for reunion of the family.

Very respectfully
Your obedient servant

Thos P. Jackson
Agent
