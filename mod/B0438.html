Freedmen's Bureau Records: David Fultz to W. H. Peyton, [1867]

Summary:
Fultz writes to Peyton, informing him of Monroe's custody claim on a boy, James.

Mr. W. H. Peyton

Richard Munroe has [unclear: consulted] me as to his right to the possession of a small boy named James, now in your possession. Richard is the brother in law, and is desirous of taking care of James. If James is not bound to you, I am of opinion you cannot retain him.

David Fultz
