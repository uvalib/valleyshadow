Augusta County: William L. Coan to Samuel Hunt, November 20, 1865

Summary:
Hardly obscuring his aggravation with the manner in which AMA administrators answered his calls for more teachers, Coan tersely asserts, "It is time I knew whether you design to supply." He states that he will be traveling to Washington, DC over the next couple of days and says he will look for a reply to his request there.

Bro Hunt Staunton Va

Nov 20th/65

I leave here for Washington D C this AM "under orders" shall be there but a few days. I trust Mr Wilson sends for me as I expected he would when Troops were to be paid off.

Write me there by return mail about teachers for Front Royal Woodstock Harrisonburg Lexington etc

It is time I knew whether you design to supply.

I shall write you from Washington

Very truly

W L Coan
