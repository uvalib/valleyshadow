Freedmen's Bureau Records: George T. Cook to Orlando Brown, October 18, 1866

Summary:
Cook asks for a five-day leave of absence to go to Washington, D.C.

Bureau Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Office Assistant Superintendent Sub-District No. 10 7th District Virginia Brevet Brigadier General O. Brown
A.A.A.G.
Richmond Virginia (through Headquarters Seventh District Virginia)

October 18th 1866

Staunton Augusta County Virginia

General:

I have the honor to request "Leave of Absence" for five (5) days to visit Washington D.C. on important private business.

I have been on duty in the "Bureau" since the 2d of April 1866 during which time I have not had a "Leave of Absence."

Very Respectfully
Your Obedient Servant

Geo. T. Cook.
1st Lieutenant Veterans' Reserve Corps and Assistant Superintendent
