Freedmen's Bureau Records: Special Requisition Order of W. Storer How, April 1866

Summary:
This requisition order by How asks for "Service Recruiting" flags and halliards for display in front of Bureau offices throughout the District to demonstrate the presence of government authority and to reassure those who support the government.

Special Requisition For Sixth District, Virginia Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands

7 Service Recruiting Flags
7 Service Recruiting Flag Halliards

I certify that the above requisition is correct and that the articles specified are absolutely requisite for the public service rendered so by the following circumstances: to be displayed at the Office of each Assistant Superintendent in this District, to indicate the presence of United States authority, and to cheer and confirm these people in their disposition "to be and become more and more constant and persevering in their [unclear: renewed] allegiance."

W. Storer How
Brevet Major & Assistant Quartermaster Superintendent

Captain, Assistant Quartermaster United States Army will issue the articles specified in the above Requisition
Commanding
Received at ________________ the _______ of ________ 1866 of Assistant Quartermaster United States Army
in full of above requisition, [signed] W. Storer How
Brevet Major & Assistant Quartermaster Superintendent
