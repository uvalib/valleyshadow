Freedmen's Bureau Records: William A. Staples to [Thomas P. Jackson], May 13, 1867

Summary:
William Staples writes on behalf of a woman who seeks the return of her son, Jack. Her son, only 15 years old, is living in Staunton and is employed without her permission. Staples' letter indicates that the woman seems to have trouble controlling her children, as the father is dead.

May 13, 1867

Near Amherst Court House Virginia

Dear Sir-

A colored woman named Izetta (formerly belonged to Mays near here) is living on my Farm and requests me to write to the authorities at Staunton and get them to make her son Jack aged 15 years and who has been at Staunton for two or 3 years come home to her at once and to collect his hire and remit it to her to my care person, had no right to employ him -- without her permission said boy's Father is not living She seems not to be able to control her Boys of whom she has several

Your obedient servant

William A Staples
