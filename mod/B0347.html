Freedmen's Bureau Records: Elizabeth Elmsly to Thomas P. Jackson, November 8, 1867

Summary:
Ms. Elmsly asks Jackson to forward any correspondence on to her present location in Albemarle County. The nature of Elmsly's relations with Jackson are unclear, except that she mentions Annie Branham, someone against whom Jackson filed a suit.

Captain Jackson

November 8th 1867

Meechum's River

Dear Sir

Should you receive a letter from Mr Elmsly or should one come to Staunton for me, to your care or to the care of Mrs Branham, please forward to me at Meechum's River, Albemarle Virginia care of M.W. Wallace Esquire. I am waiting here to hear from him before going to Baltimore. Please hand the enclosed note to [unclear: Cush--] & Peck & oblige.

Yours Respectfully

Elizabeth Elmsly
