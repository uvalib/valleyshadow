Freedmen's Bureau Records: Summary of case between Joseph Harrison and James E. Carson, June 28, 1867

Summary:
Jackson's copy of a Freedmen's court proceeding (written by Dews) shows that the court decided in the favor of Joseph Harrison, a blacksmith, in his suit against James Carson.Board of Agents met in accordance with call of Lieutenant George T. June 28, 1866

Staunton, Virginia

Board of Agents met in accordance with call of Lieutenant George T. Cook 24 Veterans' Reserve Corps Assistant Superintendent

Case of Joseph Harrison (colored) vs. James E. Carson

Case having been tried at former meeting and adjourned to await the arrival of Deposition from Jacob Thornton of Highland County Ohio, the recorder Lieutenant George T. Cook Assistant Superintendent here produced the Deposition of said Jacob Thornton.

The Court after mature deliberation upon the evidence adduced decide that the Defendant James E. Carson pay to the Plaintiff Joseph Harrison, freedman, the sum of Thirty five (35) dollars and ninety-five (95) cents with the Interest to the first of June 1866 to the amount of five (5) dollars and eleven (11) cents.

signed Wm J. Dews

True copy from original record Board of Agents February 12/66
[signed] Thos Jackson, Agent
