Franklin County: Augustus Hover to His Mother, August [1864?]

Summary:
Hover asks his mother to send him a box of supplies.

Mother

Camp Near Bel Plain Landing virginia

Dear Mother

I take my pen in hand to let you know that I am well at this present time and hope that these few lines will find you in the same state of good health. I got the paper and envelopes that you sent me and i was glad to get them for [illeg.] them. I am sorry that John was sick but i am in hope that he will get well again. i think that we will get paid off in a few days and then i will send some of it home to you i want you to send me a box if you can but if you can't it won't make much difference. Direct your box in care of cortermaster. Nothing more at the present time but write soon [illeg.]

Augustus Hover

i send you my old likeness But now is laterent round of our Regiment and i want you to keep it till get home and then i will get framed.
