Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, November 19, 1868

Summary:
Evans writes to Sibert with family news and discusses the slow mails and plans for a visit.

Nov 19th 1868

Staunton

My Dear Mollie

I looked for a letter from you this Morning my love but was sadly disappointed I wrote you by Hamie on last Monday but he did not leave until Tuesday and I felt Confident that I would get a letter this Morning from you My love I will not blame you for I Know it was not your fault for if they had of been a regular mail I Know I would gotten a letter from you. I do hope that your Pa will have

[page 2]
a Post office established when he returns home Ada received a letter from him on Yesterday he Stated in the letter that he would be home the latter part of this week provided he left New York on Monday.

My love I will be up Saturday if I live.

Magnus is talking of Coming up with me I hope he will I can't say for certain that he will come for you Know Magnus. Ada said last night that she was going to write to her Ma that I was afraid to Come up Saturday you tell

[page 3]
your Ma that I am coming if I live & that I will take all the blame on myself. My love I love you with true devotion of my heart & Soul, I love you with unchanging devotion heart soul mind & strength and Ever shall as long as I live you are the only one in this world I love I Can not Express my love for you My only love of this Earth. All are well all send love I Send a loving Kiss you. My love to your Ma Hamie to all. I love you with my whole heart

Your Loveing

D H Evans
