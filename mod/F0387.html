Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Louisa Geisel, November 7, 1862

Summary:
Geisel writes to his sister about his new activity of drilling fresh recruits from nascent units.

Louisa Geisel

November 7th 1862

Camp near Frederick, Maryland

Dear Sister Louisa

I received a letter from you some time ago, which I have neglected to answer as I have been very busy this while past. I have in my charge 25 new recruits belonging to Company L, a new Company which is being formed for our regiment, and it keeps me busy from morning until night to attend to them. In the fornoon I have to go out with them on a foot drill, and in the afternoon we have a mounted drill. This morning about 9 O'clock it commenced to snow, and it is still snowing yet, 4 o'clock [illeg.] and we will therefore not go out on drill this afternoon, so I thought I would write you a letter, although I have not much news to tell you for we are now almost in the sea of our army and hardly know what is going on in front. I wrote a

[page 1]
letter to sister Hannah a few days ago which I suppose she has received by this time. I received one letter from Amanda, which I answered when we were at Williamsport, but I have not received any letter from her since, and Anne has not written to me yet. I would be very glad to receive a letter from her and I heard that Spencer Brand was very sick. I am anxious to hear how he is by this time. We have a young fellow in Company H by the name of James [illeg.] and I don't doubt but what you know him and there is one in Company L named Flechenstein, he used to live at the cowpath road, perhaps you know him, and Charles Johnson the Blacksmith, who used to live in Line Lexington is in Company M but neither of them knew me any more. Charles Johnson lived at [illeg.] square and was drafted, and as soon as he was drafted he enlisted in this regiment. he has been here a little over a week.

[page 2]
I have not heard from the 104th Pennsylvania since we came into Maryland; if you have heard from any of them, I would be glad to hear how they are getting along and where they are. has Sergeant Mathis gone to his regiment yet? I got my Photograph taken at Frederick and enclosed you will find one if you will please to accept of it. I must now bring my letter to a close, hoping that it may find you all in as good health as I enjoy at present. Give my best respects to all enquiring friends. my compliments and love to all the family at home. I remain

Your affectionate brother

Christian Geisel

Company H 6th Pennsylvania Cavalry Colonel Rush's Lancer's Camp near Frederick Maryland
