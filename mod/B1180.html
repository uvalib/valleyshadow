Freedmen's Bureau Records: W. Storer How to Orlando Brown, April 22, 1866

Summary:
How requests transportation for an indigent Staunton woman to Albemarle County, where her parents will care for her.

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Office Superintendent Sixth District Virginia Brevet Brigadier General O. Brown
Assistant Commissioner
Richmond, Virginia

April 22d 1866

Winchester, Virginia

I have the honor to request that you will forward me transportation from this point to Afton, Albemarle County for Louisa Frintz, a freedwoman who is at Staunton, sick and destitute, and dependent upon the Bureau for support; she has no home here, and will be cared for at Afton by her parents who reside there.

I am, General
Very Respectfully
Your Obedient Servant

W. Storer How
Brevet Major & Assistant Quartermaster
Superintendent
