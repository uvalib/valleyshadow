Franklin County: Bob Taggart to his sister Tilly, March 18, 1863

Summary:
Taggart's brief note states that all is well and that it is unlikely he will come home soon.

Tilly

March 18 1863

Camp at Miners Hill Va

Dear Tilly

I have had a letter from home for some days now and am not certain that it has not been [deleted: over a month] answered. Will drop you a line before going to bed this morning to let you know I am well and that there is nothing new or important going on here which must be my excuse for not writing a longer letter. I received Lizzie's of the and John's of the 16th today and will answer them soon. From Lizzie's I learn that Fanny Darragh is in Pittsburgh. I suppose

[page 2]
general conduct [unclear: men ] if I should not participate in any of [unclear: the]. I may get home some of these days but cannot state the precise time. My love to all at home of course, including Fanny.

Your Affectionate Brother

Bob
