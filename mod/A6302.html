Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, May 19, 1868

Summary:
Sibert reports on his trip home and discusses family news.

May 19th 1868

Staunton

My dear Mollie

Ella & myself arrived home safely without any accident we got home at half past Eight O'Clock much sooner than I Expected Magnus was waiting patiently for us he said he had given us out we did not stay long in Bridgewater Rennie & David were very glad to see us & wanted us to stay all night they were both well I don't think Rennie is looking as well as she

[page 2]
when she was at home a few weeks ago. Ella & myself had a great deal of fun on the road she will give you a full description of our trip. I know she will make you all laugh. David Sibert wanted to trade his Cow for Macon you ought to have seen Ella look at him when he proposed to trade. David will never forget the look as long as he lives but afterwards he complimented Macon very highly. he did not mean what he said to Ella but was

[page 3]
only teasing her. I thought of you all the way home & after I got home too this last Visit just seems like a Dream to me for we did not see much of Each other it won't be the case next time I come for I will come by myself then I will not be hurried away from you my love I still love you with true Devotion of my heart I love you more & more every day of my life & never cease to love you as long as God permits me to live. I have not felt very

[page 4]
well since my return home I have had a very severe headache but better this morning. Ella & family are all well give my love to your Ma & Pa tell your Pa I wrote to him by today's Mail love to all the Children. I send love & a loving Kiss to you I love you with all my heart God Bless you good bye

Yours

D H Evans
