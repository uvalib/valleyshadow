Augusta County: Ebenezer Junkin to Francis McFarland, June 2, 1862

Summary:
Junkin writes to McFarland asking his assistance for the upcoming Communion service at his church.

June 2d 1862

New Providence Manse

Dear D r M c Farland

We have determined to have our summer communion on next Sabbath week (3 d Sab. of June). I expect to have Brother Walker with me during the meeting, which will commence on Friday; but the Session expressed a desire that I should write & ask you also to be with us. Some of your old friends wish very much to see & hear you again. Mr Morrison I know would be greatly gratified to meet you. He was out yesterday, but I did not get to speak to him.

[page 2]
I believe he is in his usual health.

Brother Harris would supply your pulpit for you I have no doubt.

Remember me kindly to Mrs. McFarland & your family. If you come bring her with you & make us a little visit. Hoping that you will be able to comply with our request.

I remain yours truly in Christ

E.D. Junkin
