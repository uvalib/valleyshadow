Freedmen's Bureau Records: Thomas P. Jackson to John A. McDonnell, December 17, 1867

Summary:
Jackson writes McDonnell about the Alexander-McCutchen case, noting the the administrator of the McCutchen estate requires legal proof before he will settle the claim.

Captain John A. McDonnell
Sub Assistant Commissioner
Winchester

December 17, 1867

Staunton, Va

Captain

I respectfully refer to a communication from Brevet Lieutenant Colonel James Johnson Sub Assistant Commissioner 6th District Virginia dated May 1/67 (returned to your Head Quarters May 12/67 with an endorsement thereon made by me. May 12/67) asking aid to collect a claim of $130 said to be due to the children of Henry Alexander (colored) by James McPhetus McCutchen, deceased, lately of Augusta County Claimant in behalf of the children of Benjamin Moten (colored)

The heirs dispute the claim and the Administrator notifies me that unless legal proof is presented he cannot pay the amount or any portion thereof. As the estate is solvent and must soon be settled, I ask Henry Alexander's children [unclear: furnish] me at once with proper legal proof of the solidity of their claim.

Very Respectfully
Yr. Obt. Servt

Thos. P. Jackson, Agent
