Franklin County: John Ault to John Stewart, January 11, 1863

Summary:
John Ault, the chaplain of the 126th Pennsylvania, requests leave because of sickness, emphasizing his greater chances of recovering with the comforts of home.

John Stewart

January 11th, 1863

Camp Near Portsmouth, VA

Sir

I have the honor respectfully to request leave of absence for three weeks to go to London, Franklin County, Pennsylvania.

My reasons for making this application is that for a week and a half I have been laboring under a severe indisposition, rendering me wholly unfit for duty. I am persuaded I shall not recover while lying in camp. The comforts and attentions of home will I hope enable me to return soon to duty.

I am very respectfully
Your Obedient Servant

John Ault, Chaplain
