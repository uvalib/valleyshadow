Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Louisa Geisel, June 18, 1862

Summary:
Geisel provides his family with an elaborate account of his maneuvers during the Seven Days Battles near Richmond.

Louisa Geisel

June 19th 1862

Camp near Cold Harbor

Dear Sister!

Having some leisure moments today i thought i would spend them in writing you a few lines to let you know that I am well and alive, and i hope that this letter will find you all in as good health as i enjoy at present. I am obliged to write this letter with a lead point for the bottle of ink i took with me from Washington is used up, and [illeg.] opportunity of getting any here, and I hope you will therefore excuse me if my writing is not [illeg.]

I received a letter from Father a few weeks ago in which I [illeg.] that some of you had [illeg.] i also received your letter of the 21rst of [illeg.], and I was very glad to hear from you [illeg.] Father a letter since i received his last one on which i suppose he has received his last one which i suppose he has received before this time [illeg.]

[page 1]
that it was at Fort [illeg.] on the James river near Richmond. i heard that our men have taken the fort with a large number of prisoners. i have not seen the official report of it yet for it takes several days before we get the newspapers from Philadelphia and consequently you always hear the particulars of the battles before we do. yesterday fighting was going on at bottom Bridge on the [illeg.] but i have not heard the result of it yet. there is fighting going on today somewhere near Richmond, but i cannot tell exactly where it is. I see by the papers that a good many of the boys of our neighbourhood have been wounded at the battle of Fair Oak. i also have three from Whitemarsh in Davis's regiment who have been wounded. one of them, his name is William Nicolous, was my messmate in the three month service. they must have had a hot time of it, but i hope they may all get over it safe. A party of rebels, who had been somewhere about Ashland since the fight at Hanover Courthouse passed through this part of the county last Friday in our rear. we got word of it at three o'clock in the afternoon and went in pursuit after them right away; we followed them up all Friday night and until saturday afternoon to the [illeg.] Creek which they had crossed just before we got there and burned the bridge behind them.

[page 2]
they destroyed a train of Wagons near Turnstall station on the Richmond Railroad. they went through there about twelve o'clock on Friday night and we arrived there about four o'clock on Saturday morning by what we could ascertain there was about three thousand of them, all cavalry. Our regimental Quartermaster, Davis, Lieutenant Marro of Company B, and one of our ambulance drivers were taken prisoners of the rebels. they had been to the Whitehouse on Friday with some sick men and were on the road back when they met the first party of rebels. We have been on picketguard since last monday. we were relieved yesterday; we were encamped near Mechanicsville when we started on picket, but when we came back to the camp yesterday we found that our regiment had moved to Cold Harbor. we followed them [illeg.] at our new Camp about one o'clock last night. It is very warm today. There are plenty [illeg.] about here, which are about getting ripe and we help ourselves to them, whenever we have time. I have not received any letters from Sister Hannah since the first one she wrote to me and i should be glad to hear from her. I must now close my letter with my kindest regards to you all, give my best respects to all inquiring friends. i should be glad to hear from you soon again. tell me all the news about the neighbourhood. do you how Renchlers are getting along; if you get to Philadelphia give them my kindest regards. is Polly Furhman married yet or have you put her down on the Bachelors list. tell her that i am well and alive yet. If we gain the battle at Richmond, the time of which is nor far off i think this war will soon come to a close, when i hope that it we all may see each other again.

You affectionate Brother

Christian Geisel
