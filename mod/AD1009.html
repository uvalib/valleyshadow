Augusta County: Diary of Robert P. Bryarly (1863-1864)

About Robert P. Bryarly:
Robert P. Bryarly kept a diary from November 1863, when he turned 20 years old, to November 1864. He titled his diary "Notes of my twenty-first year," and in it he provides accounts of his service in Company B of the 1st Virginia Cavalry. Bryarly briefly chronicles troop movements, skirmishes with the enemy, visits to relatives while on furlough, and the deaths of comrades. The diary ends with an injury in late 1864. According to a register of the Confederate Hospital at Charlottesville, Virginia, Bryarly returned to duty on April 7, 1865.

May 1st and 2d

on Guard, raining quite fast,

3d

In camp.

Yankees, Yankees crossed Rapidan. Brigade moved out on Telegraph road, towards Chancellorsville.

Had a severe fight with Yankees Infantry. had 3 men wounded my company: Corporal Seivest and Private Frieze A. J. severely and M. L. Payne slightly. In afternoon started after Sheridan's raiding party towards Richmond. had a fight with their Rear guard, marching all night. Major Woolridge of 3d Virginia Cavalry wounded.

May 10th

Fighting all day. Sergeant G. W. Marshall severely wounded (of my company) near Childsburg,

11th-

Found enemy destroying railroad at Ashland, drove him off. followed them to Chickahomminy; Had severe fight at Half Sink bridges, In which we were repulsed. We lost Major General J. E. B. Stuart, Colonel Pate of the 5th Virginia Cavalry. Also our beloved Captain G. N. Hammond, and Privates Kilmer and Stump slightly wounded. our loss in officers and was very heavy.

12th

October 1864

October 1st.

Came through Staunton to Mt. Sydney on picket and advance guard. at night on picket, Centreville.

2d

Had a skirmish at Bridgewater. Drove Yankees across river, camped for the night near Centreville.

3d

In camp until 2 o'clock then went to Bridgewater. Encamped near there for the night.

4th

Moved out near river to support picket. In evening my squadron went on picket.

5th

On picket, relieved by Second Virginia Cavalry, went to camp.

6th

Advanced to Brocks Gap, drove Yankees across north branch Shenandoah river. nearly every barn burned and same for houses. camped near there for the night.

7th

Moved on the back road. captured several wagons and a great many sheep. Encamped for the night near Forrestville.

8th

Fighting all day near Fishershill. I was severely wounded in right arm in the evening. very cold. stayed at field hospital during the night.

9th

Started for General Hospital at Charlottesville. Rosser badly whipped lost all his artillery and wagons, spent the night at Mr. Hollers.

10th

Arrived at Brigewater, spent the night at Mr. Miller's. had my arm dressed at Dayton.

11th

Came to Spring Hill. had my arm dressed by Dr. Bell. stayed with Mr. Theophilus Gamble until the 17th, (very kindly treated by his family, also by Dr. Bell,) Then started for Charlottesville, spent the night at Brookville, next came to my Uncle's in Albemarle County

20th

Went to the Hospital, wound doing well. Remained in hospital until 1st Nov. then came to Bluefield.
