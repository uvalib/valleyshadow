Augusta County: John Scott to Samuel Hunt, April 18, 1866

Summary:
John Scott asks Samuel Hunt whether funds collected from local blacks for clothing distributed by the AMA should be used to reimburse Mr. Hunt or whether those funds should be used to help pay off rent for a church Scott had acquired and turned into a schoolhouse. He says he is concerned that leaving the matter unresolved over the summer might allow the "secesh negrohaters" in Staunton to take the schoolhouse from the AMA. He also mentions that local blacks helped raise money by putting on a concert in town, and that they had arranged to have a fair on the first of May.

S Hunt Staunton Va

Apr 18/66

Dear Sir

I received a note from the rooms, I suppose as it was written on paper with the printed heading of yours, but signed S R. Montross in which letter was stated that there are [deleted: th] four boxes of clothing on the way for Freedmen

Now as it is summer almost and the people are at work I think the most of them will be able to pay a trifle on these clothes, What I wish to ask is. What I shall do with the money? Shall I hand it over to Mr Tukey as I do book money at the end of each month and then use it to mitigate your expenses? The church

[page 2]
in which we teach is not yet secure as there is $166. yet due on the first installment of $666. At your suggestion it might be applied to help pay that and would be of advantage. I dare not leave the thing so insecure to go home at vacation for no one knows what these secesh negrohaters would do The colored people think of holding a fair the first of May to raise the money and I think they will nearly do it We have just held a concert of religious pieces mostly, among the children of the day school and raised 54 dollars to help pay for the church.

J. Scott
