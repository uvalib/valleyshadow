Freedmen's Bureau Records: Frederick S. Tukey to Orlando Brown, July 17, 1868

Summary:
Tukey writes a letter recommending William Dews and attesting to his character. Tukey and Dews worked together in Staunton while Tukey was Agent there.

Bureau Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Head Quarters 2nd Division 6th Sub District Virginia Brigadier General O. Brown
Assistant Commissioner
Richmond, Virginia

July 17th 1868

Bowling Green

General

This will introduce to your favorable notice Mr. Wm J Dews of Richmond Va. A man tried and true in the Union Cause who is seeking a position in the Bureau as Agent or Clerk.

I have had a three years acquaintance with him and it affords me great pleasure to recommend him as a Man of Excellent character and business attainments.

Any favors you can show him will be worthily bestowed.

Very Respectfully

FS Tukey
