Franklin County: Lucius P. Mox to Jennie, [date unknown]

Summary:
Mox writes a brief letter acknowledging the receipt of Jennie's last letter.

Jennie

Dear Jennie

next time I write I will likely be able to give you some of my adventures in Lovettsville for I have been detailed to work in the Lov. Bakery at that place. I was down to town this afternoon and seen some very pretty ladies who will likely get acquainted with this chicken before many weeks roll around But now Jennie you must not get jealous for I am resolved not to fall in love with any of them, only just for the occasion.

You asked my advice as to how you should spend your next summer - I am really surprised to think you would ask me what [deleted: had to] to employ yourself at when you are better able to judge for yourself. you know your circumstances better than I do. So my advice is to do the best you can

[page 2]
Now Jennie dear as it is getting late you must excuse me for not writing more tonight I must go to the bakery early in the morning so good night dear girl Good night

Very Affectionately Yours

Lucius P. Mox
