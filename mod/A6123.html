Augusta County: John P. Dull to Giney Dull, October 26, 1864

Summary:
John Dull writes to his wife, Giney Dull, about the possibility of visiting his home.

October 26th 1864

Camp Lee near Richmond

Dear Giney

This evening finds me in this place well and hearty and I hope that you all enjoy the same blessing we reached this place last [deleted: tuesday] wednesday eve we are doing very well! We have all been assigned to earlys command in the Valley, and expect to be in Staunton in a few days I will try to get home if we get to town but do not know whether I can or not There are a great

[page 2]
many men here now and are going to the different parts of the army every Day, we get enough to eat on very good quarters to stay in nothing to do but eat and sleep. I would like to know how you are getting along at home I have been well as ever I was in my life since I left home but this kind of life does not suit me

trusting in god for all needed good I must close for this time

as ever yours

John P. Dull
