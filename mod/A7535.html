Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, November 12, 1867

Summary:
Evans expresses his love for Sibert and mentions plans for spending Christmas together.

Nov 12th 1867

Staunton

Miss Mollie

We [unclear: rived] home Safely met with no accident on the road we [unclear: rived] in Staunton at Eight O clock. Ella had good warm Supper waiting for us which Magnus and my Self did ample justice to Ella was glad to See us She said that She did not look for us before ten O clock I just thought to my Self if I had of known that Ella didn't Expect us before ten I would not of hurried my Self a way from you I will come by my Self next time and I won't be in Such a hurry to leave you. I liked the arrangement your Ma made about you coming up a Christmas

[page 2]
very well I could not have arranged it better my Self you will not object to the arrangement will you. two more long weeks will have to pass before I can see your dear face I feel that I can't wait until I See you again Each visit I pay you only increases my desire to be with you always I am cheerful & happy with you I love you truly and devotedly with all my heart and Nature. I was over to see your Ma. & Ella last night & passed the Evening very pleasantly all send love to you Lula says she wants to see her Aunt Mary. I sent Mr. Curtis [unclear: Some] Tobacco directed to your Ma. I hope it will please him

you must Excuse this Short note you must write me a long letter Friday give my love to Rennie & Ada I send love and a kiss to you

[page 3]
I love you truly with all my heart & Soul my very nature God bless you Good bye

Yours

David H Evans
