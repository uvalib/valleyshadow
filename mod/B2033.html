Freedmen's Bureau Records: Edward P. Smith to R. M. Manly, September 10, 1868

Summary:
Smith informs Manly that Corliss should not plan to teach at Staunton, as the post has already been filled by John Scott, a well-known former teacher there.

American Missionary Association,
No. 53 John Street,
Reverend Edw. P. Smith, General Field Agent Reverend R. M. Manly
Superintendent of Education for Virginia
Richmond, Virginia

September 10 1868

New York,

Dear Sir:

Our attention has been called to your recommendation to Mr. Corliss to occupy Staunton.

Mr. John Scott who has previously taught there, and is known favorably to the people, has already made arrangements to go there.

Very Truly Yours

Edwd P. Smith

per S.A.T.
