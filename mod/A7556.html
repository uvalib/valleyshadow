Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, March 31, 1868

Summary:
Evans writes Sibert to reassure her that he is not angry with her and still loves her.

March 31 1868

Staunton

My Dear Mollie

Your dear letter of Yesterday came Safely to hand I was Truly glad to hear of your good health The reason I did not come down Saturday was I thought you had gone away from Home & new that you would write by today's mail if you were at home & I thought again that you had mised the mail on Friday. Magnus and myself will be down Saturday if we live I am not angry [unclear: with]

[page 2]
you nor do I doubt your love for me for I know you love me with your whole heart. I love you the same Oh I do want to see you so badly that I can scarcely wait until Saturday comes I must close as its Time for the Mail to close I write you this note to let you know that I still love you with all my heart & that I am not angry with you my love to your Ma, Ham & all the children

I send love & a loving Kiss to you

God Bless you Good

[page 3]
bye I love you with all my heart & mind & soul Write soon

Yours

D H Evans
