Augusta County: Jacob H. Craun to Susan Kersh, December 7, 1861

Summary:
This letter describes conditions in camp fairly favorable and notes some sickness in the camp.

Allegheny Mountain

December 7 1861

My Dear grand ma

I am sure that you would be very glad to hear from me this is the first letter I ever wrote to you. I think I will write you a few lines to let you know how we all are we have got in our cabins they are warm and [deleted: comfortabl] comfortable we [illeg.] a great deal of work to do then we have some little work to do to our cabins I hope we will get them done before long we have been fortifying for two or three weeks we are not near done fortifying there are two regiments on the hill their [unclear: dir] on prices of common here Captain miller has four cannon there are some sick men in our regiment Virginia Volunteers The Friend's in our company are mostly [added: all] well Admiral W. kersh is well at this time I don't think he has be unwell we have plenty to Eat. I have ben sick [unclear: about] 6 weeks I am not well at this time I shall bring any letter to A close shop you will forgive my mistakes and bad writing for this time. [illeg.] Fry is we and hearty I hope when these few lines come to your hand it will find you in good health

write to me soon and give me the [added: news]

Jacob. H. Craun

Susan Kersh
