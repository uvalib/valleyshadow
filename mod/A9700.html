Augusta County: P. Brady to Alexander H. H. Stuart, March 31, 1868

Summary:
P. Brady writes to Alexander H. H. Stuart to help settle a financial dispute with Daniel Brady.

Hon. Alex H. H. Stuart

Mar 31/68

Philada

Dear Sir

I beg to say that in Case Daniel Brady will carry out his offer of Sep 3. Shortening the last four payments I will accept, giving him a full discharge from all further Claims, returning him two notes of 2500 each and a release as Guardian of my Children, he says the property of my Children passed into my hands, not so. it went to pay the debts of P. Brady & Company after I was robbed, I make the offer to accept his proposition now as I am pressed by my [illeg.] for monies due them

[page 2]
If you can get 5000 in good bonds as the proposes in June so much the better I would prefer an amicable settlement if it can [added: be] had on a fair basis, and trust to him to [added: do] more when he succeeds with his patent, it is understood thus he is to pay your fees & all charges incurred thus far

Yours Very respectfully

P Brady
