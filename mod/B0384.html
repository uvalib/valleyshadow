Freedmen's Bureau Records: J. F. Hoffman to Thomas P. Jackson, January 16, 1868

Summary:
Hoffman states that he will be coming to Staunton to settle accounts with a Richard Banheart, presumably a former employee.

January 16th 1868

Sangersville

Sir

I received your note of the eleventh in regard to Richard Banheart's work. I will come up next week and settle with him, if no providential interference I would have come before now, but his clothes is here, and I did not want to come until I could come with the wagon, and bring his clothes.

Yours Respectfully

J.F. Hoffman

Thomas P Jackson
