Franklin County: John Berryhill to Simon Cameron, November 12, 1860

Summary:
Berryhill writes in support of a clerkship assignment for the son of D. O. Gehr.

November 12th. 1860

Chambersburg,

Honorable Simon Cameron
Dear Sir

By the request of D O. Gehr Esquire I am induced, again, to claim, your friendship and interposition in behalf of his Son, Hastings, who has been recently admitted to practice Law in this place but as he is not an orator, & as it will require much time and study, to become celebrated in his profession, he inclines to a Clerkship at Harrisburg or Washington City.

I will say he is smart, writes a good hand and by a little experience will become a good accountant is moral, Steady, and amiable, and would prove a creditable acquisition in any position he may be called to act. Your attention to him will much oblige Your old friend and Obedient Servant

John Berryhill
