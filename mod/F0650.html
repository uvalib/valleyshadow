Franklin County: John Hamer to Eveline Hamer, November 24, 1863

Summary:
Hamer writes about his sickness resulting from a bad tooth. He also discusses the impossibility of women visiting.

Eveline Hamer

Nov. 24th 1863

Springfield

Dear Sister

As you know that [illeg.] I am sick and in the Hospital. I would suppose that you are anxious to hear from me how I am getting and so on. Well I cannot say that I am much better than I was when I wrote to you last although I believe I am a little better. I have still a pain in the side of my head - somewhat like a neuralgia. I think it arises from a bad tooth which must be extricated if it doesn't behave. I had, also a pain on the breast but I had a blistering plaster, - a terrible hot one, too it burned a little like Greek fire - laid on my breast and that put a stop to that. I am not confined to bed and when the weather permits I can run about through town. Emanuel [unclear: Carster] is in the hospital, too.

I received a letter from Eveline the other day. Tell her I'll write as soon as I get a little better. She said she soon be at home again. If my old red shirt is pretty good yet and you get a good chance suppose you send it down Maybe Nathaniel will take a notion to come down and bring me a Christmas gift. I wish he only could or poor us will have to do without a Christmas again. I would not invite [unclear: any] women [unclear: down] here because you could not get a place to stay over night among so many secessionists.

I would like if somebody would see after that money that I sent with Joseph Spangler ($60.00) and let me know as soon as possible if it arrived safely. If it is not at [unclear: Raineys] or [unclear: Pisels] some one had better go over to Spanglers and get it.

I hope to be able to go back to camp in a few days although my medicine keeps me [unclear: middling] weak.

Give my well wishes to my dear parents, brothers and Sisters. Write soon.

I still remain your most affectionate
Brother

John Hamer

I wrote to you on the 19th Mary Ann Hamer
