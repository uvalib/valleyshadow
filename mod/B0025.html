Freedmen's Bureau Records: F. M. Bird to Thomas P. Jackson, October 28, 1867

Summary:
Bird's letter to Jackson asks for protection for him and other men who fought for the Union during the war. Bird, a resident of Highland County, describes threats and attacks against him by men still loyal to the Confederacy.

Captain Jackson

October 28 1867

Monterey
Highland County Virginia

Dear Sir

I will State to you that since I was at your office in Staunton I have returned to my house in Highland and have gotten into a right Serious difficulty which I will State. Some of the facts. My Brother and Myself were attacked on the road by Some Rebels who threw Rocks at us and pursued us to the house and Commenced an attack upon us there. They broke open windows threw rocks into the house. They kept up the attack for some time hurraying for Rebels and Cursing yankees. My Brother was badly injured from being struck in the face with a Stone. One other man and Myself were Slightly injured the [unclear: dips]: an other union mans house was Stoned the Same night we got a warrant and had one man by the name of Ratcliff arrested he said the Reason why we were treated So was because we were claimed Radicals he gave bail for his appearance at Court but I have no idea that he will be punished and him and others I understand have been making Threats against union men. I wish to know of you if we Cant get some protection by the Military. I think after having Soldiered for the Union I deserve protection in it. I wish you to write to me immediately and let me Know what to do. I Think that I am well able to prove that David Hicks was engaged in this thing the man that you saved from punishment last summer he is now Threatening union men.

I am able to prove to you that [deleted: the] he is nor never has been a loyal man and Charges against him is Correct and he never was employed by the government and I advise you to let the civil law have its affect upon him. I am able to Substantiate what I have Said in this letter by Reliable men. Please write immediately and let me know what to do

yours truly

F.M. Bird
