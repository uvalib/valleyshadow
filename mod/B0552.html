Freedmen's Bureau Records: W. Storer How to George Q. White, December 21, 1865

Summary:
How writes about how best he may procure his needed supplies.

Captain Geo. Q. White Assistant Quartermaster
Chief Quartermaster T.F.A. Richmond Virginia

December 21st 1865

Winchester, Virginia

Captain

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your communication of the 18th in Reference to Requisitions, and in reply I have respectfully to state that I have not construed the order referred to in the manner which I now see is implied.

Respecting the requisitions already approved I have to communicate the information that stores can probably be most readily obtained from the Depot at Harpers Ferry, as I understand there is an accumulation of Quartermaster Property there under the care of Brevet Major Geo A. Flagg Assistant Quartermaster (from whom the transportation can certainly be best procurred) and I think he can better furnish the wood which I thought to get delivered at the offices of the several Assistant Superintendents and have it paid for in [unclear: Form 12] by Maj. Flagg, who would then issue it to me. I have therefore respectfully to request that the approved requisitions may be forwarded to me in order that I may attempt in this way to obtain the much needed stores.

I am &c

WSH
Captain &c
