Freedmen's Bureau Records: W. Storer How to Frederick S. Tukey, December 8, 1865

Summary:
How asks Tukey to estimate how much wood he will need for distribution to those too poor to afford wood during the winter, even though, as How points out, he does not know whether or not he will be able to procure any.

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands Mr. F. S. Tukey
Sub-District B
Staunton, Virginia

December 8th 1865

Winchester

Sir

Please make an Estimate of the quantity of wood that will be needed for gratuitous issue to the very poor freedmen of your sub-district during the next three (3) months.

I have none on hand and cannot speak encouragingly of the prospect of obtaining any but desire you to make a careful estimate in view of the possibility that an immediate supply may be obtained.

State any facts within your knowledge relating to present or prospective suffering for want of fuel.

Any of the articles likely to be wanted in the next quarter, Stationery &c must now be Estimated for, as my estimates must be forwarded on the 15th of this month for Quarter ending March 31st 1866.

I am &c

W.S.H.
Captain &c

Copies to all the Assistant Superintendents
