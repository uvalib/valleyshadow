Freedmen's Bureau Records: James H. Skinner to Orlando Brown, October 10, 1865

Summary:
Skinner writes regarding an order about the restoration of property belonging to his mother, Clara Skinner, in Norfolk. Both, however, reside in Staunton.

Colonel O. Brown Assistant Commissioner Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands

October 10th 1865

Staunton

Sir

I enclose the [unclear: Memorial] of Mrs. Clara W. Skinner and the instructions thereon of Major General Howard. I doubt not you will find pleasure in issuing your orders in conformity therewith for the restoration to Mrs Skinner of her house & lot in Norfolk City. I will authorize some friend of hers in Norfolk to call at the office of the Freedmen's Bureau in that city and [unclear: take] possession for her.

Very Respectfully

James H. Skinner
