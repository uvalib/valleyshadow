Augusta: Franklin Carroll to Virginia Miller, August 23, 1864

Summary:
Carroll wishes to know if his correspondent received his previous letter and asks if anyone has interfered with his last mailing.

Mrs Virginia L Miller.
Little york
Pennsylvania

August 23 [added: 1864]

Camp Chase Ohio

Mrs Virginia Miller

having wrote to you some time ago in regard as to some clothing and as yet Have not received them and have come to the conclusion you did not get [deleted: it] [added: the letter] I also have learned that another man had wrote to you also I Thought in all probability he had interfered when I wrote to you there were no prisoners in this camp knew your address But myself and the very Evening I wrote To you I understand That there was another letters wrote I want you to write to me and let me know if you got my letters or not and if that man who you received a letters from interfered or not you will please answer This letter soon and let me know what you can do in regard as to my wants with these few

[page 2]
lines I close Hoping to hear from you soon if you send me these clothing Send them in care of Colonel Richardson commanding & By Express

please answer this soon

Yours Truly

Franklin Carroll Prison B Camp Chase Ohio

P S please send me some stamp
