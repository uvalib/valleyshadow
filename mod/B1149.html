Freedmen's Bureau Records: John W. Jordan to Orlando Brown, July 11, 1868

Summary:
Jordan requests a leave of absence to see his brother, who he reports was in an accident.

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Office 4th Division, 9th Sub-District, Virginia. Brevet Brigadier General O. Brown
Assistant Commissioner &c
Richmond Virginia

July 11th 1868

Staunton, Virginia,

General

I have the honor to request "Leave of Absence" for Ten (10) days from this Post for the purpose of visiting a younger brother in Chicago Illinois who has recently met with a terrible accident and whom I have not seen for years.

In this connection I desire to call your attention to the fact that this is only the second time I have requested leave of absence to go beyond the limits of this District since being assigned to duty in this state, January 26 1866

I am General
Very Respectfully
Your obedient Servant

Jno. W. Jordan
Assistant Sub Assistant Commissioner &c
