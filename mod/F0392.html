Franklin County: Christian Geisel to Louisa Geisel, May 20, 1863

Summary:
Geisel writes his sister about a dramatic cavalry raid by his unit into the area around Richmond.

Louisa Geisel

May 20th 1863

Camp near Brooks Station

Dear Sister

Being now settled in campaign for a few days I embrace the present opportunity to write you a few lines hoping that they may find all in as good health as I enjoy at present. I received your welcome letter of the 3rd [illeg.] on the 13th while on picket near Hartwood Church and I was very happy to hear from you. We had a short but successful campaign as far as cavalry is concerned from April 29th to the 9th of May; it lasted but a short time, much shorter than I expected when we started, but long enough for us to be going night and day getting but little rest or sleep and to do a good deal of destruction in rebeldom. I had some idea of where we were going when we started but I would not have believed that we could have gone as near to Richmond

[page 2]
as we did without encountering and being opposed by some portion of the rebel army. We captured a number of prisoners on our march but they were all in small parties and could make no resistance; we crossed and recrossed the Rappahannock, Rapidan; North Anna + South Anna, the three former we had to ford and in our return the Rappahannock was so swelled from the rain that our horses had to swim; all of our Regt. got across safe, I only heard of one man being drowned, the South Anna R. had a bridge across. The people in the vicinity of Richmond did not know what to make of it that we were down there and the rebel army up at the Rappahannock, with all their communication and supplies cut off and i have no doubt Jeff Davis himself was alarmed about his his safety in Richmond. It is any belief that we could have made a dash into Richmond and destroyed it and by what I can learn Gen. Sherman would have done it if he would have been

[page 3]
supported by Gen. Averell, who was to go to Gordansville, and failed to do so. We destroyed all the railroad and telegraph running North from Richmond, captured several trains of cars, and wagon trains, which were all burned up, and a large quantity of army stores. A squadron of the 5th U. S. belonging to our Brigade had a scrimmish near Thompsons crossroads in which they lost a few men but the rebels soon took to their heels. We stopped at Thompsons crossroads on the 4th of May, I got some writing there over a hundred years old, I will enclose a page for curiosity. We lost two men of our Co. one was our [illeg.]Workman, his horse gave out near Louisa Courthouse and our Capt. Cook fell out of the ranks near the same place, they both got in the rear and did not join us again, and I expect were taken prisoner. we have not heard from them since. I got through safe myself, lost my old horse and two caps going through the woods at night but I had the good luck to get both another horse and cap again without expense.

[page 4]
My horse gave and the second day after we crossed the Rappahannock, near the Rapidan with the sore back, the first time I ever had that misfortune, but I managed to get a young secesh in place of him who answered the purpose very well. From the 29th of April to the 9th of may we only had our horses[illeg.] three hours, after the provisions had run out we carried along we lived on ham and corncakes of which we found a large quantity stored away in some places, we found at one house over 2,000 Hamas stored away; there is no able bodied man to be seen [illeg.] through Va. they have all been drafted in the army, and the people down there are getting tired of the war.

I expect we will stay at this camp, until we get filled out again with things that got lost and broke on the raid and many of our horses are worn down so that they are unserviceable for which we will have to get other ones. No other movement has as yet commenced in this part of the army that I know of, everything is quiet. The weather is beautiful. I must now come to a close with only my best respects to all our family and all inquiring friends, hoping soon to hear from you again.

I remain your affectionate Brother

Sergt. Christian Geisel [illeg.]

Co.H 6th Penn. Cavalry Army of the Potomac. Va.
