Augusta County: William H. Hanger to John Miller, December 9, 1864

Summary:
Hanger writes to his cousin, the Reverend John Miller, asking to borrow several dollars.

Rev John Miller
York Pennsylvania Fort Delaware

December 9th 1864

Dear Cousin

for the first time since I have been a Prisoner I take the present opportunity of writing you a few lines to let you know where I am & how I am I am well at the present and have been a prisoner since the 12th of May last and I stand in need of many little articles which I cannot get without the money and would like very much if you would send me a few dollars if you can spare it conveniently and I will pay you back if it is ever in my power. the folks were all well at home when I last heard from them when you write direct to
William H. Hanger
Company E 5th Virginia infantry
Prisoner of War at Fort Delaware
Division Number 4
in care of Captain G.W. Ahl on attached envelope

William H. Hanger

[on reverse of letter] Reverend John Miller York Pennsylvania
