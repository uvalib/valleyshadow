Freedmen's Bureau Records: John K. Keiser to Thomas P. Jackson, February 20, 1868

Summary:
Keiser asks Jackson to help him to evict three hired people whose term is up.

Mr Th. P. Jackson

February 20th 1868

Waynesboro

Dear Sir

You did not come on tuesday as you promised me. Those negroes are still in there and want possession of the house. As I told I gave them 3 weeks notice one I hired only by the week and settle up with her the other one has been staying there she has good opportunities to hire for more than I gave her but she thinks I think that they will draw a support from the government but she is able support herself you will accomodate me by removing them immediately and you oblige

yours &c

John K. Keiser
