Freedmen's Bureau Records: W. Storer How to Orlando Brown, February 14, 1866

Summary:
How informs Brown that he will be travelling through part of the District, ending up in Staunton.

Bureau Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Head Quarters, Sixth District Virginia Colonel O. Brown, Assistant Commissioner
Richmond, Virginia

February 14th 1866

Winchester, Virginia

Colonel

I have the honor to inform you that I shall start today on a tour through the upper portion of my District, reaching Staunton, perhaps, on the 17th of the month, and respectfully request that any business requiring immediate attention may be directed to me at that place until about the 24th of this month.

Captain G. W. Wells, agent and Assistant Superintendent will [unclear: transact] the remaining office business here until my return.

I am Colonel
Very Respectfully
Your Obedient Servant

W. Storer How, Captain & Assistant Quartermaster
Superintendent Sixth District, Virginia
