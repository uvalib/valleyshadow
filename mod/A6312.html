Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, August 4, 1868

Summary:
Evans discusses Sibert's decision not to visit Staunton, reports family news, and makes plans for a future visit.

August 4th 1868

Staunton

My Dear Mollie

Your dear and welcome letter of the 30th came safely to hand and was read with pleasure. I do not blame you for not coming to Staunton. I did not think you would come for I know it would not suit you to pay Staunton a visit at this time I think I know your reason for not coming and if it is what I think it is I don't blame you for not coming I would rather you would not come although I would like for you to come. I often think of the pleasant walks we had last summer and the pleasant Evenings we spent together they were indeed happy

[page 2]
hours to me. I have often sat alone and pictured to myself the pleasant walks we would have this summer in Staunton and the pleasant Evenings we would spend in Each other society but alas that can not be unless your Pa moves to Staunton which I hope he will do and then it would be much more pleasant to visit you at your own home. I know you are anxious to come Staunton my love. I think it would be best for you to postpone your visit until you move here which hope won't be long. I am very sorry to inform you that it will be impossible for me to pay you my regular visit next Saturday if I live I will be down Saturday a week I am sorry to disappoint you my love but Business prevents me from

[page 3]
going. Dr Arthur told me that he was coming down with me Saturday a week I will let you know for certain in my next letter if he is coming. oh how much I would like to See you this morning my love one who I love with unchanging Devotion of heart & soul and one who I love more than all others on this Earth

Ella was taken very sick on Yesterday She is much better this Morning. I hope your Ma is Entirely recovered. My love to your Ma & Pa love to all

I send a loving kiss to you and all the love of my heart my love is only for you my love God Bless you Goodbye

Yours

David H Evans
