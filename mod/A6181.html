Augusta County: David H. Evans to Mary Anna Sibert, February 9, 1869

Summary:
Evans writes Sibert urging her to come to Staunton Thursday. He discusses family news, his love for her, and his return from his last visit.

Febry 9th 1869

Staunton

My Dear Mollie

Home Safely again. My love I will look for you down on Thursday for certain with your Pa & Di don't disappoint us for we will look for you. Ella was so much disappointed this morning that you & Di did not come I will be at the train Thursday morning My love if I live. I would liked of Stayed longer with you if I could have done so for when I go to see you I always

[page 2]
dislike to leave you for I would rather be with you my love at your home than any other place in the world. I thought this morning when I Started I would be left. I ran nearly all the way to the Gap I had plenty time.

I will look for you down Thursday my love for certain. I love you with the same unchanging devotion of my heart & Soul & ever Shall while I live. I love you my love & no other in this would you were my first & only love of my heart & Soul

I can't write you but a Short note this time my love after I got home I had some Business to attend to that Keep me busy till nearly car time. give my love to your Ma & Pa Di Lolo & Ham tell Lolo I will answer her question & then I have one to ask her My love come Thursday for certain. I will look for you I love you with my whole heart & Soul mind & Strength.

Ella & family are all well Send love to you all & will look for you all Down Thursday. I Send a loving Kiss to you my love & love you with my whole heart.

Yours Loving

D H Evans
