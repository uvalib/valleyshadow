Freedmen's Bureau Records: John W. Jordan to John A. McDonnell, May 16, 1868

Summary:
Jordan asks McDonnell for an appropriation of twenty dollars for the Waynesboro Freedmen's Schoolhouse, which McDonnell declines in the attached endorsement.

Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands,
Office 4th Division, 9th Sub-District, Virginia. Captain John A. McDonnell
Sub Assistant Commissioner
Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen and Abandoned Lands
Winchester, Virginia

May 16th 1868

Staunton, Virginia,

Captain

I have the honor to apply for an additional appropriation of Twenty $20 Dollars for the benefit of the Waynesboro Freedmens School House etc. for the following named reasons.

The building is very nearly completed & all that is wanting now to [unclear: reuse] same is a matter of 4000 Shingles for the purchase of which the appropriation is called for.

I earnestly hope the appropriation will be made as there is no possibility of our being able to raise the amount required from any other source.

I am Captain Your obedient Servant

John W. Jordan
Assistant Sub Assistant Commissioner &
