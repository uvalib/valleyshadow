Augusta County: George Wils(?) to Mollie Wils(?), January 28, 1861

Summary:
The writer expresses the desire to come home from school.

Mosey Creek Academy Virginia

Dear Sister

Your came to hand this evening and the contents were noticed very closely. I suppose Green & his darling will go down to Strasburg to live I wish him a happy time Mollie I will most certainly comply with your request that of not answering [deleted: your] her letter, I am not anxious to answer her letter if I get one from you. I must differ with you about [unclear: Lollie] thinking anything of me I am

[page 2]
sure she does not but we will soon see. I know you had a nice time out at W.G. you could not help it for I know it is a nice place to visit, I think [unclear: Pet] has abandoned the thought of writing to me or is too much taken with [unclear: Dr] so [unclear: he] can't find any time you would like me home saturday. I may be there and if I had money enough I think I would come sooner. I am sure Ma will let us come and if she does look for us on Saturday. you must write Wednesday night and get her to add a note and the money for us to come home, use your influence for us once try and persuade her to let us come don't forget to write at the time above mentioned whether we come or not for we don't want to start on friday until we get [deleted: an] [added: definite ] answer,

Your Brother

George
