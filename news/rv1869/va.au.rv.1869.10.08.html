Staunton Vindicator: October 8, 1869

Go To Page : 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |

-Page 01-

-Page 02-

[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The Augusta County Fair is approaching and the paper urges Staunton's citizens to work hard to prepare for the large crowds from Augusta and adjoining counties that are expected. "The ladies can do more in less time than gentlemen and we shall expect to see the exhibiting rooms splendidly adorned with the work of their hands. Ladies of Augusta see to it that you win the laurels which Augusta's sons so greatly delight in seeing you wear."

Augusta County Fair
(Column 01)
Summary: The paper prints the program distributed by the executive committee for the Augusta County Fair.
(Names in announcement: John B. Baldwin, William A. Burke, E. W. Bayly)
[No Title]
(Column 01)
Summary: The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore accepted the invitation of the Mayor and Council of Staunton to visit during the fair.
[No Title]

Summary: William A. Burke has been appointed agent for the Virginia State Agricultural Society.
(Names in announcement: William A. Burke)
[No Title]
(Column 02)
Summary: A daily train will run between Charlottesville and Staunton during the fair.
[No Title]
(Column 02)
Summary: The Staunton Musical Association will give a concert at the chapel of the Deaf, Dumb, and Blind Institute. They will perform the cantata "The Haymakers."
[No Title]
(Column 02)
Summary: Mr. Scheffer, outgoing proprietor of the Virginia Hotel, held a farewell oyster supper.
(Names in announcement: Scheffer)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: S. M. Taylor of Staunton and Miss Rebecca Brown of Augusta were married near Swoope's Depot on September 30th by the Rev. William E. Baker.
(Names in announcement: S. M. Taylor, Rebecca Brown, Rev. William E. Baker)
Married
(Column 02)
Summary: Joseph M. Kunkle and Miss Fannie A. McCutchen, daughter of the late Robert McCutchen, were married at Shemariah on September 22nd by the Rev. H. Gilmore.

Married
