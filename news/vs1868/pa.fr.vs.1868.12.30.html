Proclamation By the President
(Column 01)
Summary: Andrew Johnson issues a proclamation of general amnesty to supporters of the late Confederacy.
Virginia and the Carolinas, No. 2
(Column 01)
Summary: Ongoing account of a trip made by a group from Pennsylvania and Maryland through Virginia and the Carolinas.

-Page 03-

Concert
(Column 01)
Summary: The Union Sabbath School of Fayetteville will hold a Brass Band concert on New Year's Eve. Admission is 20 cents.
Exhibition and Concert
(Column 01)
Summary: Greenvillage will hold an exhibition and concert on New Year's Eve including charades, tableaux, solos, duets, and a haunted house.
Lecture
(Column 01)
Summary: The Young Men's Christian Association are sponsoring a lecture at the Court House. Prof. J. H. Shumaker will deliver his address on "Manliness."
(Names in announcement: J. H. Shumaker)
Monumental Fair
(Column 01)
Summary: The Monumental Fair earned a profit of $200, much less than expected. The paper blames the result on hard financial times.
Parade
(Column 02)
Summary: The Housum Zouaves paraded in the streets of Chambersburg and maneuvered on the Diamond on Christmas Day.
Chambersburg Female Seminary
(Column 03)
Summary: The Rev. William G. Hawkins will open the second term of his Female Seminary. Hawkins "during the First Term devoted himself to his pupils so completely as to win their highest esteem. They all speak well of his qualifications both of head and heart." The school also has a Primary English Department for pupils of both sexes.
(Names in announcement: Rev. William G. Hawkins)
