Reconstruction of Virginia
(Column 01)
Summary: Looks with alarm to Radical policies concerning Virginia's readmission. Gives the example of an order signed by a Virginia governor having to be approved by a general before becoming effective. Believes such measures signal a real threat to liberty and states rights and pleads with the people to recognize this and protest against it.
Full Text of Article:

We learn, by a telegram from Washington, that the House Reconstruction Committee has come to the conclusion that the Virginia Legislature had no authority to proceed to the election of Senators until its members had taken the test oath. Of course this conclusion is simply reached, and will be voted on, to please the dissatisfied Radicals who were beaten in the election in Virginia. Congress will go to the extreme of regarding that election as a nullity. We should not be surprised if they would, after mature deliberation, be inclined to declare that Walker was not elected Governor and that Wells was the successful candidate. After what we have seen already, we are prepared for anything.

The telegram states further that it is believed that Congress will sustain this view of the Committee.

Mr. Hoar gave his opinion as Attorney General that the taking of the test oath was not necessary. General Grant agreed with him in that opinion and sanctioned the idea of electing United States Senators before knocking at the door of Congress. After all this, could there be an exhibition of worse faith on the part of the Radicals than to "go back" on these opinions, and keep Virginia in a condition of military subjection and the whole country in a state of agitation, for the attainment of base political ends?

We were never so struck with the degradation of a sovereign State as we were a few days ago. A fellow stole a horse in this county and was traced in Virginia, where the horse was recovered, and the thief arrested and lodged in jail. The District Attorney of this County procured a requisition from Governor Geary on the Governor of Virginia for the delivery of the offender. We saw the warrant of Gilbert C. Walker, issued in response to this requisition and authorizing the delivery of the horse-thief to the agent appointed to receive him, and this warrant, after having been signed by the Governor, was marked "approved" and signed to the "Brevet Brigadier General, commanding the First Military District of Virginia." And this in a Republic. The Governor of the "Mother of Presidents" is actually so powerless and so completely shorn of the dignity of his office, that he is obliged to submit such an official document to a military commander before it is of any binding authority within the State limits. And this almost five years after the "war for the suppression of the rebellion closed!"

The people must get wakened up to the point of saying to our military Chief Magistrate, and our despotic Congressional Directory, that there must be an acknowledgment of "the rights, dignity, and equality of the States," or another war. If they do not, encroachment will follow encroachment, acts of tyranny and usurpation will follow each other in rapid succession, until all State rights will be entirely swept away or ignored, and the liberty of the citizen be made to depend upon the will of a man, whose arbitrary disposition inclines and whose education leads him to regard and trust other men as mere machines, fit only to work out his purposes. Oh, for a general awakening to a proper appreciation of the sacredness of personal liberty and the rights of the States before it is forever too late! Such legislation as that which characterized the last Congress, and which bids fair to be followed by the present Congress, is enough to damn any country, and if the people fail to rebuke it, it will be no wonder if an overruling Providence shall see fit to hand this nation over to the horrors of anarchy and another civil war, or to the dominion of a despot who will ride rough-shod over the liberties of the people.

Lecture of Bishop Simpson
(Column 02)

Donations
(Column 02)
Summary: A group of 100 ladies and gentlemen presented the Rev. Hays, pastor of the Central Presbyterian Church, with a large number of Christmas gifts including clothing and toys for his children.
